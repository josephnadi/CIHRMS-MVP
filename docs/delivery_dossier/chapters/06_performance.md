# Chapter 6 — Performance Management

> *In one paragraph.* Performance Management is the institute's annual rhythm of expectation, observation, evaluation, and correction — turned into software. Every employee in CIHRMS has, or can have, a written performance contract for the cycle with weighted KPIs, an ongoing set of goals with periodic check-ins, one or more reviews (self, manager, peer, skip-level, upward) feeding a 1-to-5 rating, a calibration round-table that normalises those ratings against an agreed distribution, a 9-box that places every rated employee on a performance-vs-potential grid, and — when things have not gone well — a Performance Improvement Plan that the system insists on before any non-disciplinary termination can proceed. The gap analysis (Chapter 44) classified Performance Management as Phase-2 work; the foundation (cycles, goals, reviews, 9-box) shipped in Wave 13 and the PSC-style contracts, calibration sessions, and PIPs were completed and merged the week of 30 May 2026.

## Where to find it

- **Sidebar location:** **Performance** group (magenta colour code, "monitoring" icon). Expanding the group reveals seven entries — **Analytics** (the landing page at `/modules/performance`), **Goals**, **Reviews**, **Contracts**, **Calibration**, **PIPs**, and **9-Box**. The group is gated behind `performance.view`; the four sensitive entries (Calibration, PIPs, 9-Box, and the analytics page itself) carry their own per-permission `visible:` flags so a user without the relevant grant never sees a dead link.
- **Roles that see it:**
    - **super_admin** and **ceo** — every page, every employee, every signature, every adjustment.
    - **hr_admin** — Analytics, Goals, Reviews, Contracts (read-only on contracts unless also granted `performance.manage`), Calibration (`performance.calibrate`), and PIPs (`performance.pip_manage`). HR cannot apply a locked calibration session unless they also hold `performance.calibrate_apply` — that is the dual-control gate.
    - **dept_head / manager** — Goals and Reviews for the people in their reporting line, plus their own row. Contracts they own (as supervisor) for sign-off. 9-Box scoped to their department in Phase 1 (today it requires `performance.manage`, so most line managers cannot reach it).
    - **employee** — their own Goals page, the Reviews page filtered to "Reviewee" so they only see what's about them, the Acknowledge button on a submitted manager review, and the Contracts page filtered to their own contract for signing. They do not see Calibration, PIPs, or the 9-box.
    - **auditor** — read-only on every page in the group via `performance.view`, but no `manage` / `calibrate` / `pip_manage` powers.
- **Related modules:** Employees (Ch 3) — every contract, goal, review, calibration adjustment, and PIP hangs off exactly one `employee_id`; Leave (Ch 4) — approved leave during a cycle is visible to the reviewer through the employee detail page and is one of the inputs a manager weighs at end-cycle; Learning & Development (Ch 7) — a low rating on a competency-tagged KPI is the trigger the Phase-1 learning recommender will use; Establishment (Ch 8) — the annual step-increment job (`StepIncrementService`) reads the latest closed-cycle manager review and gates the increment on a rating ≥ 3.0, the one place in the MVP where Performance is wired to compensation; Off-boarding (Ch 9) — closing a PIP with the `failed_terminated` outcome is the documented hand-off into the off-boarding case; Reports & Analytics (Ch 31) — the 9-box matrix, rating distribution, and PIP success/termination counts feed the institute-wide leadership disclosures.

## Every button, every action

### Goals (`/performance/goals`)

| Button / field | What it does | Who can use it | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| **My / My Team / Org segments** | Three-pill ribbon. "My" is loaded by default; the other two re-fire the Inertia request with a different `employee_id` filter (or no filter for Org). For an employee without `performance.manage`, only "My" is active. | Anyone with `performance.view` | Goals are the most-visited Performance page and almost every visit starts with "what am I committed to?". The default segment answers it in one paint. |
| **Search box** ("Search goals…") | Free-text filter that fires 380 ms after you stop typing. Matches `goals.title` with a LIKE scan. | Same | Most goal lookups are by title — debounced search beats refresh-on-every-keystroke for feel and DB load. |
| **Employee picker** | Filters the list to one person. Hidden when the active segment is "My". | `performance.manage` or the dept_head viewing their reports | A manager landing on the page wants to flick through their team one person at a time. |
| **Cycle dropdown** | Filters the list to one cycle. The current active cycle is pre-selected if `?cycle_id=` isn't already set. | Anyone | Cycle is the natural slice for performance work — H1 2026, H2 2026, probation cycles, etc. |
| **Status dropdown** | Filters by `Draft`, `Active`, `At Risk`, `Completed`, or `Cancelled`. | Same | "At Risk only" is the most-asked-for view for HR and dept heads. |
| **Clear** (appears only when a filter is set) | Resets all four filters and re-queries. | Same | One-click undo. |
| **New Goal** (cobalt CTA, top right) | Opens the right-hand slide panel. Fields: employee (auto-set to self), cycle (auto-set to active cycle), title (required, ≤ 200 chars), description (≤ 5000), cadence (`annual / half_year / quarterly / monthly / weekly`), target value, current value, unit, weight (0-100), starts_at, due_at. | `performance.manage`, or the employee for their own goal (the FormRequest's `authorize()` enforces this) | Goal-setting is the entry point to the rest of the module — anything else (reviews, contracts, PIPs) builds on the goals an employee committed to at the start of the cycle. |
| **Goal card → Check-in** | Inline form, posts to `POST /performance/goals/{goal}/checkins`. Fields: progress %, current value, narrative (5-5000 chars), mood (green / amber / red). The service writes a `GoalCheckin` row, mirrors `current_value` back onto the parent goal so cards refresh, and **auto-flips the goal status to At Risk** if mood is amber or red on an Active goal. | `performance.manage` or the goal's own employee | Mid-cycle check-ins are how the system catches a slipping goal early — the mood field is intentionally three colours, not five, so the choice is unambiguous. |
| **Goal card → Edit** | Re-opens the slide panel with the row pre-filled; PATCHes `/performance/goals/{goal}`. Setting status to Completed also stamps `completed_at = now()` if it wasn't already set. | `performance.manage` or the goal's own employee | Targets shift mid-cycle; the panel handles that without an extra page. |
| **Goal card → Delete** | Confirm dialog → `DELETE /performance/goals/{goal}`. Soft-delete via the `SoftDeletes` trait. | Same | Same one-way safeguard as employee delete; the row is recoverable from the console. |
| **Pagination** (bottom) | Standard 20-per-page; `withQueryString()` so filters persist through page changes. | Anyone | Keeps the page snappy on institutes with thousands of goals per cycle. |

> *Notes:* The controller's `goals` action eager-loads `employee.user`, `cycle`, and the single most-recent check-in per goal, so the card grid renders without an N+1. The "progress %" you see on each card is the model's derived `progressPct()` attribute — it is **not** stored, it is computed from `current_value / target_value` on every render so a stale `progress_pct` column can never lie to you. Ownership of the check-in and update endpoints is enforced twice: at the `authorize()` layer of the FormRequest and again as a defensive `abort_unless()` inside `PerformanceController` (added in the audit-v2 tier-3 supplement, item 27) because the parent route is gated on `performance.view`, which is broader than "I am the subject of this goal".

|---|---|---|---|
| **Reviewee / Reviewer / All tabs** | Three-pill tab strip. "Reviewee" filters to reviews about me; "Reviewer" filters to reviews I wrote; "All" removes both filters. Non-managers only see "Reviewee". | Anyone with `performance.view` | The natural two perspectives an employee has on the page — and the third is HR's view. |

| **Rating distribution histogram** | A five-bin client-side histogram of `overall_rating` (rounded) across the loaded rows. | Anyone | Visual cue that the cohort's rating mix is normal — a healthy distribution looks like a small bulge at 3-4; rating inflation looks like a wall at 5. |
| **New Review** (cobalt CTA) | Opens the right-hand slide panel for capturing a new review. Fields: cycle (defaults to active), employee, reviewer (auto-fills with the current user when type = `self`), type, three 1-5 sliders (overall, performance, potential — performance and potential drive the 9-box, overall is the headline number that calibration acts on), three large textareas (strengths, opportunities, comments). | `performance.manage`, or the signed-in employee for their own self-review (the `StoreReviewRequest::authorize()` checks both `employee_id` and `reviewer_id` match the user) | Self-review is the first half of the conversation; manager / peer / skip-level / upward fill in the other voices. |
| **Submit** | PATCH `/performance/reviews/{review}/submit` — flips status to Submitted, stamps `submitted_at`, fires `ReviewSubmitted` event. The reviewer must be the user submitting, or have `performance.manage`. | Reviewer or `performance.manage` | Two-stage save (Draft → Submitted) means a reviewer can park a half-finished review and come back to it. |
| **Acknowledge** | PATCH `/performance/reviews/{review}/ack` — flips status to Acknowledged, stamps `acknowledged_at`. Only the subject employee can press it. | The subject employee only | The acknowledgement is the one piece of evidence the system has that the review conversation actually happened — required for the rating to count toward calibration. |
| **New Cycle** | Opens a small panel for creating a new `ReviewCycle`. Fields: name ("H1 2026"), cadence (annual / half_year / quarterly / probation), starts_at, ends_at (must be after starts_at), three optional due dates (self / peer / manager), status (defaults Draft). | `performance.manage` | Cycles are the calendar spine the rest of the module hangs off. |
| **Close Cycle** | PATCH `/performance/cycles/{cycle}/close` — flips status to Closed, stamps `closed_at`. | `performance.manage` | Closing the cycle is what makes its reviews eligible for the annual step-increment job (see "How it talks to other modules"). |

> *Notes:* `(cycle_id, employee_id, reviewer_id, type)` is unique on the `reviews` table — the same reviewer cannot file two separate manager reviews on the same employee in the same cycle, which prevents accidental double-rating during calibration. The 9-box page uses **only** submitted/acknowledged reviews; drafts are invisible to it. `ReviewSubmitted` is dispatched on submission so downstream listeners (e.g. notifications, analytics) can react without the controller knowing about them — today no listener is registered for the event, but the wiring is in place.

### Performance Contracts (`/performance/contracts`)

| Button / field | What it does | Who can use it | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Filter strip** (search, cycle, status) | Same shape as the goals filters. Search is a cross-driver LOWER LIKE against `employee_no` and the joined `users.name`. | Anyone with `performance.view` | The contracts list is the cycle-management surface — HR uses the cycle + status filter to find every "Active" contract due for evaluation. |
| **New Contract** (cobalt CTA) | Opens a slide panel for drafting a contract. Fields: cycle, employee, optional supervisor (when blank, the employee's manager from the org chart is used at sign-time), and a KPI list. Each KPI carries `id`, `name`, `weight` (0-100), `target` (≥ 0), optional `unit`, optional `scorecard` quadrant (one of `financial / customer / process / learning`). The service refuses a contract whose weights do not sum to exactly 100. | `performance.manage` only | The PSC framework (Public Service Commission, Performance Management Policy Framework, Cabinet April 2015) requires a written, signed contract with weighted KPIs at the start of every cycle. This is the panel where it gets written. |
| **Send for signature** | Workflow button shown only on Draft contracts. Refuses if KPIs are empty. Flips status to **Pending Signature** so the employee and supervisor can sign. | `performance.manage` | One-click hand-off to the two signatories. |
| **Revoke to draft** | Shown only on Pending-Signature contracts with no signatures yet. Returns the contract to Draft so KPIs can be amended; once any signature is recorded the button disappears and the contract has to be Cancelled instead. | `performance.manage` | The "I sent it too early" undo — without this, a typo in the KPI weights would force HR to cancel and recreate, losing the linkage. |
| **Sign contract** | Workflow button shown to the employee and supervisor whose names are on the contract. Stamps `employee_signed_at` or `supervisor_signed_at` (whichever applies to the signer), and once **both** are stamped, auto-transitions the contract to **Active** and stamps `finalised_at`/`finalised_by`. | The named employee or the named supervisor only | The two-signature gate is the audit-defensible record that both sides agreed to the KPIs before the cycle started. |
| **End-cycle evaluation form** | Shown only on Active contracts. One numeric "actual" input per KPI (any value ≥ 0). On submit the service walks every KPI, computes `score_i = min(100, max(0, actual_i / target_i × 100))`, then a weighted overall `Σ(weight × score) / Σ(weight)`, writes the score and overall back to the row, and transitions the contract to **Achieved** (≥ 60 %) or **Missed** (< 60 %). | `performance.manage` plus a fresh 2FA challenge | End-cycle evaluation is the moment the cycle "closes" for that employee; the dual gate (permission + 2FA) raises the bar because the resulting number drives step increments, calibration adjustments, and (Phase 1) compensation decisions. |
| **End-year note** | Optional 5000-char textarea on the evaluation form. Saved to `end_year_note` on the contract. | `performance.manage` | A narrative companion to the number, for the staff file. |
| **Mid-year note** | Same shape, on the `mid_year_note` column. The column exists; the editing UI for it is Phase 1 work. | `performance.manage` | The PSC cycle includes a mid-year review; the data model is ready for the form. |

> *Notes:* `(cycle_id, employee_id)` is unique on the `performance_contracts` table — the `draft()` service uses `updateOrCreate()` keyed on that pair, so re-drafting for the same cycle replaces the working copy rather than producing duplicates. Weighted-achievement scoring is deterministic — the same `actuals` array always produces the same overall, with no randomness, no rounding drift, and no manager override on the formula. Manager override on the final rating is what **Calibration** is for, and it happens on the `Review` row, not on the `PerformanceContract`.

### Calibration (`/performance/calibration`)

| Button / field | What it does | Who can use it | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sessions list** | Paginated list of calibration sessions across all cycles. Each row shows the cycle name, department (or "Org-wide"), facilitator, status (Draft / In Progress / Locked / Applied / Cancelled), and the count of adjustments captured. | `performance.calibrate` or `performance.calibrate_apply` | A facilitator landing on the page wants to pick up where they left off. |
| **New Session** | Opens a slide panel. Fields: cycle, optional department (blank = org-wide), optional target distribution (defaults to the Ghana public-service 10/25/40/20/5 split — 10 % at 5★, 25 % at 4★, 40 % at 3★, 20 % at 2★, 5 % at 1★). | `performance.calibrate` | Calibration sessions are scheduled, not ad-hoc — opening one starts a clock. |
| **Distribution-vs-target chart** | Five-row chart on the session detail page. For each band (5★ → 1★) it draws the target percentage in dimmed navy and the actual percentage in either amber (over target) or emerald (at or under target). | Anyone with view rights on the session | The visual answer to "is this manager rating their team too generously?" — the most important question the room needs to ask. |
| **Reviews-in-scope table** | Lists every manager-type review with a non-null `overall_rating` in the selected cycle (filtered by department when the session is scoped). Each row shows the employee, the original rating, the adjusted rating (when one has been entered), the reason, and an "Adjust" form. | Anyone with view rights | The working surface — every review the facilitator might want to nudge. |
| **Adjust** (inline form, per row) | Captures `adjusted_rating` (1.00-5.00, the service clamps it) and an optional reason (≤ 1000 chars). Posts to `POST /performance/calibration/{session}/adjust`. The service writes a `CalibrationAdjustment` row keyed by `(session_id, review_id)` (using `updateOrCreate` so re-adjusting overwrites in place), stamping the original rating, the adjusted rating, the reason, and the adjuster. Refuses if the session is not In Progress. | `performance.calibrate` | The whole point of the session is the adjustment record; original-side-by-side-with-adjusted is the audit trail. |
| **Lock session** | Workflow button shown on In Progress sessions. Stamps `locked_at`, flips status to Locked, freezes adjustments. | The facilitator | Lock is the "we've debated enough, freeze the decisions" signal — and the gate for the dual-control apply. |
| **Reopen** | Workflow button shown on Locked sessions only (refuses on Applied). Returns the status to In Progress and clears `locked_at` so adjustments can be edited again. | The facilitator | One more "we noticed an error in the locked sheet" undo — but only before Apply, after which Review rows have already been written. |
| **Apply** | Workflow button shown on Locked sessions. Walks every adjustment in the session and writes `adjusted_rating` back into `reviews.overall_rating` in a single transaction. Stamps `applied_at`, `applied_by`, flips status to Applied. | `performance.calibrate_apply` + a fresh 2FA challenge, **and** the applier MUST be a different user from the facilitator (the service throws a DomainException if they are the same). | Dual control. The facilitator (typically an HR business partner) sets the adjustments; a second user (typically the HR director or CEO) carries them through. This is the most consequential write in Performance Management — every rating that drives a step-increment or a PIP trigger has been through this gate. |

> *Notes:* `CalibrationService::actualDistribution()` is a pure read — it always computes the live distribution from the underlying reviews, so the chart never drifts from the data. The default 10/25/40/20/5 split is a constant on the service (`DEFAULT_DISTRIBUTION`); Phase 1 surfaces a per-cycle override in the cycle edit form.

| Button / field | What it does | Who can use it | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PIPs list** | Paginated list with status filter and three stat tiles at the top — Open PIPs (open + in_progress + extended count), Succeeded YTD, Terminated YTD. | `performance.pip_manage` for the list, plus the subject employee for their own | PIPs are the most-sensitive workflow in Performance Management — keeping the list scoped to the people who need to act on it is intentional. |

| **Check-in** | Required note (5-2000 chars), required "met target" boolean. Appends to the JSON `checkins` array. Bumps `extensions_used`, recalculates `target_end_date`, appends an `[EXTENSION] reason` entry to the check-ins, and flips status to **Extended**. | `performance.pip_manage` | One-time extension is the typical Ghana practice — more than that and the org has decided to keep employing the person; the cap prevents the PIP becoming a perpetual sword. |
| **Close** | Outcome (Succeeded / Demoted / Terminated / Cancelled), required summary (10-5000 chars), 2FA-fresh challenge. Stamps `actual_end_date`, writes the summary. Refuses to close an already-terminal PIP. | `performance.pip_manage` + 2FA | The 2FA gate is the dual-control echo — closing a PIP as "terminated" is the most consequential write in the module, and the right point to insist on a fresh challenge. |

> *Notes:* `triggered_by_review_id` is the back-reference from a PIP to the rating that caused it — Phase 1 surfaces this as a button on the manager's view of a low-rated review ("Open a PIP for this employee"). Closing with `failed_terminated` is the documented hand-off into Off-boarding (Ch 9); the linkage is by employee_id today, and Phase 1 makes it a direct foreign key so the off-boarding case automatically references the PIP file. The check-ins blob is JSON rather than a child table to keep PIP detail rendering a single query — there are typically ≤ 10 check-ins on a 90-day PIP and the audit trail value comes from the chronological narrative, not from per-check-in querying.

### 9-Box (`/performance/nine-box`)

| Button / field | What it does | Who can use it | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cycle picker** | Re-fires the request for a different cycle. Defaults to the active cycle. | `performance.manage` | The grid is cycle-scoped — a Future Leader last cycle may have slipped to Core Player this cycle, and that's exactly the conversation the chart exists to surface. |
| **3×3 grid** | Rows are potential (high / medium / low), columns are performance (low / medium / high). Each cell carries a canonical label, a one-sentence prescription, a zone tint, and the list of employees who landed in it. Bucketing is fixed: Low ≤ 2.33, Medium 2.34-3.66, High ≥ 3.67. | Anyone with view rights on the page | The 9-box is the institute-wide talent map; the prescription text on each cell is deliberately concrete ("Stars: highest potential AND highest performance. Plan succession.") so the conversation in the room has a starting point. |
| **Employee chips** | Each cell renders its employees as small chips with name, position, department, and the two computed averages. | Same | Naming names is the point — anonymous bubbles in a 9-box are pretty graphs that do not move careers. |

> *Notes:* The placement is deterministic and computed per render — there is no stored `nine_box_cell` column, so a re-rated employee (after a calibration adjustment is applied) moves automatically. Drafts are excluded — only submitted and acknowledged reviews contribute. An employee with no rated review for the selected cycle does not appear on the grid at all (rather than landing in Risk by default, which would be misleading).

## The data behind it

CIHRMS stores performance data in seven core tables, all keyed off `employee_id` and `cycle_id`:

- **`review_cycles`** — the calendar spine. Name (e.g. "H1 2026"), cadence (`annual / half_year / quarterly / probation`), start and end dates, three optional due dates (self / peer / manager), status (`draft / active / closed`), the user who opened it, the timestamp it was closed.
- **`goals`** — the working units. Belongs to an Employee and (optionally) a Cycle, supports parent/child cascades (`parent_goal_id` references the same table — that's the KPI-cascade column waiting to be wired into the Phase 1 cascade UI), title, description, cadence, target value, current value, unit, weight (0-100), status enum, start and due dates, completed timestamp. Soft deletes.
- **`goal_checkins`** — the audit trail. Goal id, user id, progress %, current value, narrative, mood (`green / amber / red`), recorded-at timestamp. No soft deletes — check-ins are append-only.
- **`reviews`** — the rating records. Cycle id, employee id (subject), reviewer id (the User filling it in), type (`self / manager / peer / skip_level / upward`), three independent 1.00-5.00 ratings (overall, performance, potential), three text blocks (strengths, opportunities, comments), status (`draft / submitted / acknowledged`), submitted-at and acknowledged-at timestamps. Unique on `(cycle_id, employee_id, reviewer_id, type)` so the same reviewer cannot stack two manager reviews on the same person in the same cycle.
- **`performance_contracts`** — the PSC contract record. Cycle and employee, optional supervisor, status enum with seven values, a JSON `kpis` blob carrying the KPI list (`{ id, name, weight, target, unit, scorecard, actual, score }`), an optional JSON `balanced_scorecard` blob, a decimal `weighted_achievement`, two timestamp signatures (`employee_signed_at`, `supervisor_signed_at`), the drafter / finaliser users, and mid-year / end-year note text columns. Unique on `(cycle_id, employee_id)`.
- **`calibration_sessions`** — one row per session. Cycle, optional department, status enum (`draft / in_progress / locked / applied / cancelled`), facilitator user, three timestamps (opened, locked, applied), applier user, JSON `target_distribution`, free-text notes.
- **`calibration_adjustments`** — one row per (session, review) pair. Both ratings as `decimal(3,2)`, optional reason, the adjuster user, the adjusted-at timestamp. Unique on `(session_id, review_id)` so re-adjusting the same review in the same session overwrites the previous nudge rather than producing two adjustment trails.
- **`performance_improvement_plans`** — one row per PIP. Employee, optional `triggered_by_review_id`, opener user, optional mentor employee, status enum (Open / In Progress / Succeeded / Extended / Failed Demoted / Failed Terminated / Cancelled), opened/target-end/actual-end dates, extensions used + max, two JSON blobs (target metrics, check-ins), an outcome summary text column. Soft deletes.

What every reader of the screen needs to keep in mind:

- **The KPIs JSON blob on a contract is not a child table.** It is intentional: a Performance Contract is a *signed document* at a point in time, and the KPIs ARE the contract. Editing them after signature would be like editing a signed PDF — the system refuses. The "kpis" column carries the agreed list; the `actuals` array posted at evaluation is merged in at scoring time but the original weights, targets, and names are never re-keyed.
- **The 1.00-5.00 scale is shared by three different ratings on the Review row.** `overall_rating` is what calibration acts on and what the step-increment job reads; `performance_rating` and `potential_rating` are the two axes of the 9-box. A manager review with only `overall_rating` filled in will appear in calibration but not on the 9-box.
- **Dual control on calibration is enforced in the service, not in the policy.** The `apply` policy method only checks the permission. The "applier must differ from facilitator" check is a hard `DomainException` thrown by `CalibrationService::apply()` — defensive depth, because the permission alone is not enough.
- **PIPs are append-only on check-ins.** Once a check-in is added, the only way to remove it is a soft-delete of the entire PIP row. This is deliberate — the check-in log is the evidence the employee was given a fair chance, and editing it out after a termination would defeat the purpose.

## How it talks to other modules

- **Step Increment job (Ch 8)** → `App\Services\Establishment\StepIncrementService` runs nightly and reads the most recent closed-cycle manager-type review per employee. If `overall_rating ≥ 3.0` the step bumps by 1; otherwise the anniversary still rolls forward (so they're re-evaluated next year) and the step stays put. This is the **only** automatic link from Performance to compensation in the MVP — promotions, bonuses, merit pay, and grade changes are all still manual decisions outside the system today.
- **`ReviewSubmitted` event** → fired the moment a reviewer presses Submit. No listener is registered today; the wiring exists so that Phase 1 can hang a "new review awaiting your acknowledgement" notification on the subject employee without changing the controller.
- **`GoalCreated` event** → fired the moment a goal is saved. Same shape — wired but unlistened. Phase 1 picks this up for the cascade-suggestion engine and for the analytics rollup.
- **Calibration → Reviews** → applying a locked calibration session writes adjusted ratings directly back to the `reviews.overall_rating` column in a single transaction. The 9-box and the step-increment job re-read the underlying review, so a calibration adjustment moves an employee's 9-box placement and step-increment eligibility automatically on the next render / nightly run.
- **PIPs → Off-boarding (Ch 9)** → closing a PIP as `failed_terminated` is the documented hand-off; the off-boarding case today is opened manually with the PIP's outcome summary pasted in, and Phase 1 makes it a direct foreign-key linkage.
- **Performance Contract → Reviews** → today the contract's `weighted_achievement` and the reviews' `overall_rating` are independent. Phase 1 introduces a "auto-populate manager review from contract evaluation" path so that an employee who is Achieved on their contract starts with a 4-star draft manager review.
- **Employees (Ch 3) detail page** → the Overview tab on a person's detail page reads through to their goals, reviews, and (Phase 1) their open contract and any active PIP, so a manager can see the whole picture without bouncing between tabs.

## What's missing in the MVP

A short, honest list — every item below is in the Phase-1 backlog (Chapter 46):

- **No 360 / multi-source feedback aggregation.** The `ReviewType` enum has the slots — `peer`, `skip_level`, `upward` — and the storage works. There is no orchestration that *asks* the system to send a 360 packet to the four peers, two direct reports, and one skip-level a given employee nominated; today every review row is created one-at-a-time by a human pressing "New Review". Phase 1 adds the campaign/invitation layer.
- **No KPI-cascade UI.** `goals.parent_goal_id` is in the schema; the relationships are wired in the model (`parent()` and `children()`); no page surfaces them yet. So today the cascade is a paper exercise.
- **No mid-year contract review form.** The `mid_year_note` column exists; the end-year evaluation form exists; the mid-year form is Phase 1.
- **No compensation / promotion linkage beyond step increments.** The step-increment job is the only automated wire-up. Bonuses, merit pay grids, and promotion eligibility flags driven off the 9-box are all Phase 1.
- **No manager dashboards beyond the analytics page.** The institute-wide Performance Analytics page exists; per-manager span-of-control with rating distribution is Phase 1 work in the Reports & Analytics module (Ch 31).
- **No automatic PIP suggestion.** A review whose rating falls below 2.5 does not automatically prompt the manager to open a PIP. Phase 1 surfaces a button on the manager's view of a low-rated review.

## Standards touchpoints

- **PSC Performance Management Policy Framework (Cabinet, April 2015)** — performance contracts with signed KPIs for Public Service Organisation heads are mandatory under this framework. CIHRMS extends the same pattern to every employee. The migration's docblock cites this directly.
- **Balanced Scorecard (Kaplan & Norton, 1992)** — KPIs carry an optional `scorecard` quadrant of `financial / customer / process / learning`, and the contract detail page renders the quadrant pill next to the KPI name. The validation rule on `StoreContractRequest` enumerates exactly those four values.
- **ISO 30414 §4.4 (workforce productivity)** — every metric on the Performance Analytics page (hires velocity, headcount by department, department efficiency, average resolution hours, top resolvers) is one of the disclosures recommended under §4.4. The companion analytics chapter (Ch 31) feeds these into the institute-wide ISO 30414 report.
- **ISO 30414 §4.5 (leadership)** — the 9-box matrix, span-of-control (Phase 1), and rating distribution per manager are §4.5 disclosures. The 9-box is the placement chart the standard explicitly suggests for leadership identification.
- **Ghana Labour Act 2003 (Act 651) §17, §63** — non-disciplinary termination requires documented prior notice and improvement opportunity. The PIP module is the documented audit trail; the migration's docblock cites the section directly. Closing a PIP as `failed_terminated` is what the Labour Act expects to see in court if the termination is challenged.
- **Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843) §17, §18, §40** — performance data is personal data. The system relies on the employment-contract basis under §17, applies data-minimisation under §18 (calibration adjustments record the *reason* alongside the rating change, so the rating change is justified by data not by hunch), and supports the right-to-erasure under §40 through the soft-delete on every Performance table — true erasure is handled by the separate redaction job (Ch 26).
- Forward to Chapter 44 (Standards Benchmark) for the line-by-line mapping of each Performance Management deliverable against PSC, ISO 30414, and Act 651/843 clauses.

## What's planned next

Phase 1 of the government-grade roadmap (8-10 weeks, see Chapter 46) lands six things directly on these screens: (1) a **360 campaign engine** that, given a cycle and an employee, creates the self / peer / manager / skip-level / upward review rows in one shot and tracks completion; (2) a **KPI-cascade UI** that lets a manager nominate a goal as a child of their own and lets HR see the resulting tree; (3) the **mid-year contract review form**, writing to the `mid_year_note` column already in the schema; (4) a **PIP trigger button** on every low-rated review (≤ 2.5) that pre-fills the Open-PIP panel with the triggering review id and a sensible default duration; (5) **per-manager dashboards** with span-of-control, rating distribution, and a personal calibration view so a department head can see how their cohort compares before walking into the calibration room; (6) a **compensation linkage** that reads the contract's `weighted_achievement` and the latest review's `overall_rating` into the Phase-1 merit-pay grid (Ch 19), so an Achieved contract with a 4-star manager review becomes a recommended 5 % merit increase rather than a manual spreadsheet exercise.
