# Chapter 21 — Loans & Advances

> *In one paragraph.* Loans & Advances is CIHRMS's staff lending desk. Employees apply against a curated product catalogue (salary advance, emergency, personal, education, vehicle, housing); HR or Finance approves under dual control; Finance disburses, and from that moment the loan rides the monthly payroll run — one scheduled installment claimed per period, balance ticking down to zero, repayment paid off automatically. Termination doesn't leave a loose end either: any outstanding balance is netted against the off-boarding final settlement in the same database transaction that closes the case.

## Where to find it

- **Sidebar location:** **Loans** (top-level item, `request_quote` icon). Visible only to roles that hold `loans.view` or `loans.apply` — that excludes line managers and most operational staff by default.
- **Roles that see it:**
    - **super_admin** and **ceo** — everything, every loan, every product. (The policy's `before` hook short-circuits on super-admin; CEO holds `loans.view` + `loans.manage` + `loans.product_manage`.)
    - **hr_admin** — full directory + product catalogue management. Can apply on behalf of any employee, but cannot approve their own initiations (`approve` is not in the hr_admin grant in the seeder).
    - **finance_officer** — full directory + apply + **approve** + **disburse**. Finance is the dual-control countersignature on every loan and the only role that can release funds.
    - **employee** — `loans.apply` only. They see their own loan list (via `LoanAccountPolicy::view` falling back to `employee.user_id === user.id`) and can submit a new application. They never see other employees' loans.
    - **manager / dept_head** — *no* default access. The chain of command does not approve loans in this design; HR and Finance do.
- **Related modules:** Employees (Ch 3) — every loan hangs off `employee_id`; Payroll Engine (Ch 19) — the monthly run claims and posts scheduled repayments; Off-boarding (Ch 10) — outstanding balance is settled out of the final settlement; Audit Logs (Ch 24) — *not yet wired in* (see honest gaps below); Finance F1–F5 (Ch 20) — journal entries for disbursement and repayment are *not yet automatic* (see honest gaps).

## The data behind it

CIHRMS stores three tables for loans, plus a guarantor-pivot stub:

- **`loan_products`** — the catalogue. Code, name, type, amount window, term window, annual rate (decimal 4 dp), amortisation method, optional max DTI ratio, guarantor / collateral flags, approvals-required count, effective-from / effective-to dates, active flag, soft-delete. Six demo products ship in `DemoLoanProductSeeder`: Salary Advance (0% interest, 1 month, straight-line), Emergency (8% reducing, 3–12 months), Personal (18% reducing, 6–48 months, guarantor required), Education (12% reducing, 12–60 months, guarantor required), Vehicle (16% reducing, 24–60 months, guarantor + collateral), Housing (14% reducing, 60–240 months, guarantor + collateral).
- **`loan_accounts`** — one row per loan agreement against an employee. Reference (`LOAN-YYYY-NNNNN`, year-scoped sequential generated by `SequenceService::next("loan:{year}")` so concurrent applications cannot collide), employee FK, product FK (nullable — survives product deletion), status (enum), principal, term in months, `booked_interest_rate` and `booked_amortization_method` (snapshotted from the product at booking — a later product edit never re-prices a running loan), the calculated `monthly_installment`, `total_interest`, `total_repayable`, the live `outstanding_balance` and `installments_paid`, the workflow timestamps (`applied_by/at`, `approved_by/at`, `disbursed_by/at`), the dates (`first_repayment_period`, `expected_end_period`, `actual_end_date`), purpose, rejection reason, notes. Soft-deletes.
- **`loan_repayments`** — one row per scheduled installment. Loan FK, installment number (1..term), `due_period` (always YYYY-MM-01), `scheduled_amount`, `principal_portion`, `interest_portion`, `balance_after`, `paid_amount`, status (`scheduled` / `paid` / `missed` / `deferred` / `waived`), `payroll_run_id` and `payroll_line_id` (filled in when payroll claims and posts the row), `posted_at`, notes. Unique constraint on `(loan_account_id, installment_no)`. Inserted in bulk at disbursement; mutated only through `LoanService::postRepayment` (called from payroll's run approval) or through off-boarding's settlement netting (which waives all remaining scheduled rows in one update).
- **`loan_guarantors`** — pivot of `(loan_account_id, employee_id)` with `has_consented` and `consented_at`. The `LoanAccount::guarantors()` relation exists; no UI surfaces it yet (no guarantor-add panel, no consent-collection flow).

What every reader of the screen needs to keep in mind:

- **The booking snapshot is sacred.** When an application is filed, the calculator runs with the product's *then-current* rate and method, and those values are written to `booked_interest_rate` and `booked_amortization_method` on the loan row. If Finance edits the product the next day, every running loan keeps its original terms; only new applications see the new rate. The Audit tab's "Interest Rate" tile shows the booked rate, not the catalogue's current rate. This is the right behaviour for fair lending under Borrowers and Lenders Act §11 — the rate disclosed at application is the rate the employee owes.
- **Disbursement is the irreversible step.** Up to "Approved", a loan can be rejected with no schedule, no outstanding balance, no payroll claim. The moment Finance clicks Disburse, the full amortisation schedule lands in `loan_repayments`, the loan flips to `disbursed`, and the next payroll run for the relevant period will pull the first installment into a payslip line. There is no "un-disburse" button — a mistaken disbursement is corrected through Off-boarding-style settlement or a Finance journal entry, not a status flip.
- **Repayments are payroll-only.** There is no "Pay extra" button, no "Manual repayment" form, no MoMo-driven additional payments. Every repayment is `LoanService::postRepayment(repayment, payrollRunId, payrollLineId)`, called from `PayrollService::approve()` for every scheduled installment whose `due_period` matches the run's period. Early repayment, top-ups, and lump-sum payoffs are not built (see "Honest gaps").
- **Status flips are guarded by service-layer invariants.** `approve()` throws if the loan isn't `pending_approval`; `reject()` throws if the loan isn't `pending_approval`; `disburse()` throws if the loan isn't `approved`; `postRepayment()` is idempotent on already-paid rows (it returns the loan as-is, doesn't double-credit). The status enum is the contract; the service enforces it.
- **Dual control means applicant ≠ approver, not 4-eyes-by-role.** The policy and the service both refuse the case where the same user applies and approves. They do not — yet — require approval by two distinct users (e.g. HR review then Finance countersign). The current implementation is "any Finance officer with `loans.approve` who is not the applicant". When HR applies on behalf, the *HR user* is `applied_by`, so a Finance officer (who is naturally not HR) can approve cleanly.

## How it talks to other modules

- **`LoanApproved` event** → fired at the end of every successful approve. Picked up by `FanOutWebhooks` and broadcast as `loan.approved` to webhook subscribers (reference, employee_id, principal, term_months, monthly_installment). No other listener; no internal side-effect.
- **`LoanDisbursed` event** → fired at the end of every successful disburse. Currently has no listeners — no automatic finance journal posting, no notification, no analytics breadcrumb. The event is declared and dispatched for future use.
- **`LoanFullyRepaid` event** → fired by `postRepayment()` when the final installment is posted (balance hits zero or paid count hits term). Currently has no listeners — same story.
- **Employees (Ch 3)** — every loan FK's `employee_id` on Employee. The employee directory's profile detail page does not yet show a "Loans" tab; that's a planned addition. The `Employee` model has no convenience `outstandingLoans()` accessor today.
- **Payroll Engine (Ch 19)** — the two-phase integration. At **calculate time**, `PayrollService::collectLoanRepayments($employee, $run)` finds every `LoanRepayment` whose `status = scheduled`, whose `due_period` equals the run's period (first of the month), and whose loan is `activeForRepayment` (i.e. `disbursed` or `repaying`). The rows are *not* mutated — they're pulled into the line's `breakdown.loans.lines[]` as `{loan_reference, installment_no, amount, repayment_id}`. At **approve time**, the run iterates each calculated line's `breakdown.loans.lines`, looks up the `LoanRepayment`, and calls `LoanService::postRepayment($repayment, $runId, $lineId)`. That method flips the row to `paid`, sets `paid_amount = scheduled_amount`, `payroll_run_id`, `payroll_line_id`, `posted_at`, then row-locks the parent loan and decrements `outstanding_balance` by the scheduled amount, increments `installments_paid`, transitions to `repaying` (from `disbursed`) or `paid_off` (when balance ≤ 0 or paid count ≥ term). If the run is rolled back, the postRepayment is rolled back with it — same DB transaction. This means the payroll module also enforces Labour Act §70 limits on net pay via its deduction floor (gross × 1/3 take-home minimum); loan repayments respect that floor and can be partially applied with the remainder deferred. See Chapter 19 for the deduction-priority chain and the floor mechanics.
- **Off-boarding (Ch 10)** — when a final settlement is approved, `OffboardingService::closeOutstandingLoans()` row-locks every loan with status `disbursed` or `repaying` for that employee, marks every remaining `scheduled` `LoanRepayment` as `waived` with a note pointing back to the settlement, and updates the loan to `paid_off` with `outstanding_balance = 0` and `actual_end_date = today`. The "Outstanding Loan Balance" line on the settlement card is computed by `OffboardingService::outstandingLoanBalance($employee)` summing the `outstanding_balance` of those same active loans. The netting is automatic; there is no separate "settle loan" button — finalising the off-boarding settlement *is* the loan settlement.
- **Finance F1–F5 (Ch 20)** — **no automatic journal entries**. Disbursement does not post a journal (Debit Loans Receivable / Credit Cash). Repayment posting from payroll does not post a journal (Debit Cash / Credit Loans Receivable, plus interest income). Loan settlement during off-boarding does not post a journal. The hooks exist (`LoanDisbursed`, `LoanFullyRepaid` events) but no listener is wired. This is a real gap and a Phase 2 priority — see "Honest gaps" below.
- **Audit Logs (Ch 24)** — the loan service currently writes nothing to the audit-log table. The status transitions are recorded on the row itself (timestamps + actor FKs), but there is no append-only, tamper-evident log entry per action. Webhook fan-out for `loan.approved` is the only durable external breadcrumb.
- **Webhooks** — `loan.approved` is the only loan event broadcast today. `loan.disbursed`, `loan.repayment.posted`, and `loan.paid_off` are roadmapped (the events exist on the PHP side; the dispatcher mapping is the missing piece).

## Standards touchpoints

- **Borrowers and Lenders Act, 2008 (Act 773) — interest-rate disclosure, fair lending.** The product catalogue is the institutional rate schedule, the apply flow shows the annual rate before submission ("Personal Loan — 18.00% p.a."), and the booking snapshot guarantees that the rate disclosed at application is the rate the employee owes for the life of the loan. The full repayment schedule (principal, interest, balance after) is visible to the borrower on the loan's detail page from disbursement onwards — Act 773 §11 requires this disclosure and the system makes it permanent. The act's *cooling-off* and *advertising disclosure* requirements are not implemented in the apply flow (no statutory wording on the panel, no cancellation window). See Chapter 44.
- **Bank of Ghana Lending Guidelines — affordability and DTI.** The product schema captures `max_dti_ratio`, but the apply flow does **not enforce it**. There is no salary-to-installment ratio check at application time, no "you already have N outstanding loans" rule, no minimum tenure rule (e.g. must have completed probation). The Payroll module's deduction floor (net ≥ gross × 1/3) provides a runtime safeguard against over-collection from a payslip, but it cannot prevent the loan being approved in the first place. This is the single largest gap in the module — see "Honest gaps" and Chapter 44.
- **Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) — credit data as personal data.** Loan amounts, balances, and repayment history are personal data and are scoped accordingly: the directory only returns loans the viewer is entitled to (`LoanAccountPolicy::view` falls through to "the row is yours") and the loan detail page does not expose any data outside the employee + the actor's RBAC scope. The Act's §40 right-to-erasure provisions are not loan-specific — soft delete on `loan_accounts` is the operational default; true erasure of historical lending data conflicts with audit-retention requirements and would be handled through the redaction job in Chapter 26.
- **IFRS 9 — financial-instrument classification.** Loans are originated assets (held-to-collect), classified at amortised cost. The `loan_accounts.outstanding_balance` and the per-installment `principal_portion` / `interest_portion` split are the data foundation for an effective-interest revenue recognition schedule. The schedule is generated and stored; the **revenue-recognition journal entries are not posted** (see Finance integration above). The data is there for the auditor; the GL movements are not.
- **Labour Act, 2003 (Act 651) §70 — limits on deductions from wages.** Payroll's deduction floor of gross × 1/3 (default, configurable on `PayrollService` construction) protects the employee's take-home from over-collection in any one month. Loan repayments respect the floor and can be partially applied with the remainder deferred (see Chapter 19's deduction-priority chain).

Forward to Chapter 44 for the full standards mapping.

## Honest gaps

This is what the team should know before claiming "Phase 2 loans is done":

1. **No affordability / DTI enforcement.** `max_dti_ratio` is captured per product but never checked. An employee earning GHS 2,000/month can apply for and have approved a Personal Loan with a GHS 1,800 monthly installment — and the *only* thing that will protect them at payroll time is the Labour Act §70 floor. Bank of Ghana lending guidelines require pre-approval affordability checks; this is a Phase 2 must-do.
2. **No "no outstanding loans" check.** The same employee can hold an active Personal Loan, an Education Loan, and a Vehicle Loan simultaneously. The catalogue's guarantor / collateral flags are also captured but never enforced — there is no guarantor-consent UI and no collateral-upload flow.
3. **No minimum-tenure check.** A brand-new hire on day one can apply for and receive a 60-month housing loan. Public-sector lending typically requires 6–12 months of confirmed service before any non-emergency loan is eligible.
4. **Single-step approval, not configurable.** `approvals_required` is in the schema (defaults to 2 = dual control) but the apply flow is "applicant ≠ approver" — *one* countersigner, full stop. A future multi-step chain (HR review → Finance approval → Director sign-off for amounts over a threshold) is not built. The 2FA challenge is the second safety net, not a second approver.
5. **No automatic finance journal entries.** Disbursement, repayment posting, settlement netting, and write-offs all happen without touching the General Ledger. The `LoanDisbursed` and `LoanFullyRepaid` events exist but have no listeners. The auditor's question — "show me how the loans-receivable balance on the trial balance ties to the sum of outstanding balances on the loan-accounts table" — currently has no automated answer. This is the most operationally critical gap.
6. **No audit-log entries.** Status transitions (apply, approve, reject, disburse, post repayment, settle) write nothing to the tamper-evident audit log. The row-level timestamps and actor FKs survive on `loan_accounts` itself, but a deletion or hand-edit would erase them. The Auditor-General pack in Chapter 24 will need a hook.
7. **No early repayment, top-up, or restructure.** Once disbursed, the schedule is fixed. An employee who wants to pay off early, make an extra MoMo payment, or refinance from a 36-month at 18% into a 60-month at 14% has no in-product path — Finance has to write a manual journal and then hand-update the loan row.
8. **No default handling.** The `LoanStatus::Defaulted` and `LoanStatus::WrittenOff` cases exist in the enum but no service method transitions a loan into them. There is no "30 days past due → Missed → 90 days past due → Defaulted" job, no `loan_repayments.status = missed` recovery flow.
9. **No guarantor workflow.** The `loan_guarantors` pivot exists, the relation is wired on the model, but there's no UI to nominate guarantors, no consent-collection flow, no notification to the guarantor, no "your guarantee is being called" path when a loan defaults.
10. **No employee-facing reports.** There's no "my outstanding balance" tile on the employee dashboard, no PDF statement of account, no schedule export. The detail page covers it, but the cross-module visibility is missing.
11. **No deduction-limit safety at apply time.** Payroll's gross × 1/3 floor saves the employee *per pay period*; it does not save the institute from approving a loan whose monthly installment, combined with the employee's other active loans and statutory deductions, will cause every single subsequent payroll run to defer.
12. **`loans.disbursed` webhook event is missing.** Only `loan.approved` is broadcast; the disbursement (the moment money moves) is silent to integrations.

The module is genuinely useful for the simple case — Salary Advance, Emergency Loan, Personal Loan to a stable senior employee — and the data model is comprehensive enough that fixing the above is mostly listener-wiring and FormRequest hardening, not a re-architecture. But until items 1, 5, and 6 land, the chapter should not claim "Bank of Ghana–compliant lending".

## What's planned next

Phase 2 of the government-grade roadmap (8–10 weeks, see the gap analysis) takes this module from "structured loan-tracking" to "compliant staff lending":

1. **Affordability gate** — a `LoanEligibilityService` invoked from `ApplyLoanRequest::after()` (Laravel 10+ `after` validation closure) computes the employee's net basic salary minus statutory deductions minus existing loan installments and refuses the application if `(this_installment + existing_installments) / disposable_income > product.max_dti_ratio`. Also enforces no-outstanding-loans rule when set on the product, and a minimum confirmed-service-months rule pulled from the employee's `hire_date`.
2. **Finance journal hooks** — three new listeners on `LoanDisbursed`, the existing `postRepayment` path (via a new `LoanRepaymentPosted` event), and the off-boarding `closeOutstandingLoans` path. Each posts a balanced journal via `JournalService` to the Loans Receivable, Cash, and Interest Income accounts mapped on the loan product.
3. **Audit-log hook** — every status transition (apply, approve, reject, disburse, post, default, write-off, settle) writes an `audit_logs` row with actor, action, before/after JSON, and a hash-chained signature so a Phase 3 Merkle-tree extension can prove non-repudiation.
4. **Manipulable schedule** — early repayment, top-up, and restructure paths. Early repayment computes the next-installment-only credit (no interest-pre-payment penalty in the MVP), top-up regenerates a new amortisation tail from today's principal and rate, restructure cancels the running schedule and creates a successor `LoanAccount` linked by `parent_loan_id`.
5. **Default detection job** — a daily scheduled job marks `LoanRepayment` rows whose `due_period` is past and status is still `scheduled` as `missed`, escalates the loan to `defaulted` after a configurable threshold (default 90 days past due), and fires a `LoanDefaulted` event that notifies the employee, the guarantors, and Finance.
6. **Guarantor workflow** — a panel on the application for nominating up to two guarantors from the employee directory, a notification + acknowledgement flow with click-through consent ("I, [name], understand I am being asked to guarantee a GHS X loan to [employee]…"), and a "call the guarantee" path that creates a deduction line on the guarantor's payroll when the loan defaults.
7. **Employee self-service surface** — a "Loans" tab on the Profile portal (Ch 16) showing outstanding balances, next repayment date, full schedule, and a "download statement" PDF button. The dashboard tile follows.

The data model is largely ready for all seven; the bulk of the work is service-layer, FormRequest, and Vue UI.
