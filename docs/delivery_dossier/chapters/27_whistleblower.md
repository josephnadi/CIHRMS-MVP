# Chapter 27 — Whistleblower (Confidential Disclosure Channel)

> *In one paragraph.* This is the high-severity, statutorily-protected reporting channel that CIHRMS runs under the **Whistleblower Act 2006 (Act 720)** of Ghana. Anyone — staff, contractor, vendor, or a member of the public — can disclose corruption, fraud, harassment, mismanagement, or other improper conduct without first creating an account, without giving their name, and without leaving an identity trail on the report row. The submitter receives a single one-time **tracking code**; the system stores only the SHA-256 hash of that code, so even a database administrator cannot recover it. On the other side a small, deliberately segregated group of investigators triages the case, runs interviews and document reviews, and either substantiates it, dismisses it, or refers it onward to **CHRAJ**, the **Auditor-General**, or the **Police**. The whole flow — submission, triage, investigation actions, two-way submitter messaging, closure — is recorded in append-only `whistleblower_actions` rows that double as the case's tamper-evident audit timeline. This channel is intentionally distinct from the day-to-day Complaints module in Chapter 12: Complaints handle workplace grievances through HR; Whistleblower handles the kinds of allegations that, by law, must bypass HR entirely.

## Where to find it

- **Public entry point:** `/whistleblower` — a stripped-down, **un-authenticated** page reachable without logging in. It carries no sidebar, no app shell, no logged-in chrome; it deliberately looks unlike the rest of CIHRMS so a worried submitter knows they are on the privacy-hardened lane. A companion `/whistleblower/track` page accepts the tracking code and shows case status plus the message thread with the investigator.
- **Admin entry point:** Sidebar → **System** group → **Whistleblower** (flag icon, rose accent on the masthead). Routed at `/admin/whistleblower`. The System group is reachable to the holders of `whistleblower.investigate`, `whistleblower.view_all`, `whistleblower.manage`, or any other system-level permission such as `users.manage` / `privacy.fulfill`.
- **Roles that see it:**
    - **auditor** — by seed the auditor role holds both `whistleblower.view_all` (read-only oversight lane) and `whistleblower.investigate` (segregated investigator). This is the default investigator profile out of the box because auditor sits outside the HR chain.
    - **super_admin** and **ceo** — receive the wildcard `*` permission and can therefore see the dashboard, but the policy comment makes clear that the *intent* is that even super-admin should hold `whistleblower.manage` explicitly to read case content (no `before()` shortcut in `WhistleblowerReportPolicy`).
    - **hr_admin** — **deliberately not granted any whistleblower permission** by the role seeder. The whole point of Act 720 is that HR must not be in the routing path, because retaliation pressure typically flows through HR. The audit trail of permission seeding records this absence.
    - **Everyone else (employee, manager, dept_head, finance_officer, it_support)** — can submit through the public page like anyone else but never see the investigator dashboard.
- **Related modules:** **Complaints (Ch 12)** — sibling channel for everyday workplace issues; the two are intentionally distinct tables, distinct policies, distinct permissions. **Audit Logs (Ch 24)** — every investigator action is mirrored into the per-case `whistleblower_actions` timeline. **Identity (Ch 25)** — the anonymity guarantees described below are partly cross-checked there. **DPA (Ch 26)** — disclosure content counts as sensitive personal data under DPA Act 843 §39 and is therefore encrypted at rest. **Auditor-General Report Pack (Ch 24)** — the AG pack pulls **counts only** from this module; the body of every case stays inside the investigator lane.

| **Desired outcome** | Optional, max 5,000 chars, encrypted. | Anyone | Lets the submitter frame what resolution would look like — useful especially when the case is about retaliation. |

| **Submit disclosure** (cobalt CTA) | Posts to `POST /whistleblower`, rate-limited at **6 requests per IP per minute** by the route group's `throttle:6,1` middleware. | Anyone | The whole flow's commit. Adding hCaptcha or a Ghana-friendly alternative is on the roadmap (see "What's planned next"). The submission service runs inside a `DB::transaction` so an evidence-upload failure rolls back the report row, the subject rows, and any partial evidence rows together — there are no orphan disclosures.

## The data behind it

CIHRMS stores a whistleblower case across five tables, all pinned to the `whistleblower_reports` row:

- **`whistleblower_reports`** — one row per disclosure. Public-safe fields (case number, category, severity, status, subject summary, incident date, intake source, timestamps) stay as plaintext columns; everything that touches the substance (`description`, `desired_outcome`, `incident_location`, `submitter_contact`, `closure_summary`) is cast `encrypted` and lives in the database as Laravel ciphertext keyed by `APP_KEY`. The row carries `tracking_token_hash` (SHA-256, 64 hex chars), `is_anonymous` (defaults true), and `submitter_user_id` (always NULL when anonymous — enforced both at the service layer and again at test time). Soft-delete is on, which means destruction is operationally a flag flip; physical destruction is reserved to operational/legal review.
- **`whistleblower_subjects`** — zero or more named people or entities per report. `subject_label` and `role_context` are both encrypted. An optional `linked_employee_id` lets the investigator pin the subject to a known Employee for cross-referencing during interviews.
- **`whistleblower_evidence`** — zero or more files per report. The file content lives under `storage/app/whistleblower/{report_id}/` on the **local** disk (not the public disk). The original filename and mime type are plaintext for indexing; `caption` is encrypted. `uploaded_by` is NULL for anonymous uploads.
- **`whistleblower_actions`** — the append-only investigator timeline. Every triage, status change, interview, document review, site visit, referral (CHRAJ, Auditor-General, Police), finding, and outbound message writes one row here. The `notes` field is encrypted; the `meta` JSON column carries machine-readable context such as `{from: 'submitted', to: 'triaged'}` for status changes or `{reassigned_to: 17}` for assignments. There is no `updated_at`-driven edit path; the table is treated as write-once by the service layer.
- **`whistleblower_messages`** — the two-way thread. `direction = 'inbound'` from the submitter (`posted_by = NULL`); `direction = 'outbound'` from the investigator (`posted_by = user.id`). The `body` is encrypted; `read_at` is set when the *other party* reads the message — though the read-receipt write is not yet wired in the MVP UI.

What every reader of the screen needs to keep in mind:

- **The tracking code is irrecoverable by design.** The database holds `tracking_token_hash`, never the plaintext. The intake service flashes the plaintext through the redirect session for a single display, then it is gone. There is no "email me my code" lane, no "I lost my code, can you reset it" lane, and **no will-be** such lane: building one would defeat the channel's entire promise. A submitter who loses the code can still file a new case, but the original case is unreachable from their side.
- **Anonymous means anonymous on the row.** When `is_anonymous = true` the service forces `submitter_user_id = NULL` even if the request came from a logged-in browser (proven by the test `it('forces submitter_user_id to NULL on anonymous submissions even if a user is passed')`). There is no shadow column that "actually" records the user — there is just no place to record it.
- **The report row carries no IP address, no user agent, no session id, no fingerprint, no referrer, no analytics beacon, and no request id.** The route group is wrapped in `throttle:6,1` (Laravel's standard rate limiter), which evaluates against the client IP **at request time** for the limiter cache key but does not persist that IP anywhere durable. The Laravel application log captures stack traces and HTTP context the same way it does for every route — there is no whistleblower-specific log-suppression filter in the MVP, which is a soft anonymity concern (see "Honest gaps").
- **Case content does not appear in the Auditor-General Report Pack.** The AG pack pulls *aggregate counts only* — counts by category, severity, and status. The body of a case is statutorily segregated; if the AG wants a specific case, they go through the designated investigator and CHRAJ, not through the executive report.
- **Closure is one-way.** `WhistleblowerInvestigationService::changeStatus()` throws if anyone tries to move a closed case back to an open status. The only honest path is opening a new, related case — and the related-case linkage itself is a roadmap item (see "What's planned next").

## How it talks to other modules

- **`WhistleblowerReportSubmitted` event** → fired at the end of every successful intake, before the transaction returns. No listeners are wired in the MVP — the event exists so that downstream notifications (an SMS to the duty investigator, a Slack/Teams whisper, a CHRAJ webhook) can be added without touching the submission service. The event carries only the model — there is no plaintext leakage in the event payload.
- **`WhistleblowerCaseClosed` event** → fired at the moment the investigation service closes the case. Same shape, same listener-less posture; the event is the hook point.
- **Audit Logs (Ch 24)** → the chapter-24 audit chain captures **every authenticated mutation** in the system. The triage / log-action / message / assign endpoints all run through the standard Laravel mutation path and are therefore covered by the global audit chain. **Important caveat:** the public submission endpoint runs anonymously and is deliberately *not* writing to the global audit chain — that would create exactly the identity trail the channel exists to avoid. Instead, the **`whistleblower_actions` table is the case-internal append-only audit log**, and the investigation service is the only code path that writes to it.
- **Auditor-General Report Pack (Ch 24)** → `AuditorGeneralReportPack::writeWhistleblowerSummary()` emits a CSV of `(category, severity, status, count)` for the report period. There is no case-content leakage from this surface; the AG asks the designated investigator if they need a specific case.
- **Identity (Ch 25)** → the anonymity guarantees described above are a row-level promise; the cross-module identity model in Chapter 25 confirms there is no shadow identity attached via session, browser fingerprint, or device id at the application layer.
- **DPA Act 843 (Ch 26)** → disclosure content is **sensitive personal data** under §39. The encrypted-at-rest cast is the application-layer control; the retention schedule (currently soft-delete forever) sits on the DPA roadmap for hardening to a defined retention window.
- **Complaints (Ch 12)** → distinct module, distinct table, distinct permission tree. Complaints uses `complaints.*` permissions which are seeded to `hr_admin`, `manager`, `dept_head`, every employee, and several other roles — i.e. HR is *in* the Complaints chain. Whistleblower uses `whistleblower.*` permissions which are seeded **only to `auditor` by default**, with `super_admin` and `ceo` inheriting via wildcard. The two channels are designed not to share routing.
- **Sidebar (Layouts/AuthenticatedLayout.vue)** → the "Whistleblower" item sits in the **System** section of the sidebar, alongside DPA Requests and Audit Logs. It is rendered with a red shield iconography to signal severity, not the standard cobalt module accent.

## Standards touchpoints

- **Whistleblower Act 2006 (Act 720) §3 — protected disclosures.** The eleven `WhistleblowerCategory` enum cases are the operational expression of §3's qualifying-disclosure list — corruption, fraud, criminal offence, mismanagement, harassment, discrimination, retaliation, safety, environmental, conflict of interest, and an "other" lane. The submission form's legal-notice panel cites Act 720 by name. See Chapter 44.
- **Whistleblower Act 2006 §10 — independent oversight & CHRAJ referral.** The `InvestigationActionType::ReferralChraj` action is the operational hand-off — every "Referred to CHRAJ" decision writes a `whistleblower_actions` row with the case number and the investigator's identity, and a closure of `closed_referred` records the outcome on the case row. The actual CHRAJ submission (paper, email, or API once a CHRAJ endpoint exists) is **manual today** — the system records that the referral happened; it does not yet courier the case file to CHRAJ automatically. See Chapter 44.
- **Whistleblower Act 2006 §11 — protection from retaliation.** Surfaced in the channel through the dedicated **`Retaliation`** category and the deliberate exclusion of `hr_admin` from any whistleblower permission. Retaliation cases are first-class disclosures in their own right; the segregation keeps them off any HR investigator's queue. **Note:** there is no automatic flag that *links* a retaliation report to a previously filed disclosure by the same submitter — that link is the human investigator's job and the per-case timeline is the place they record it.
- **CHRAJ Act 1993 (Act 456).** The CHRAJ referral action type explicitly names the Commission for Human Rights and Administrative Justice. The pack of referral actions (CHRAJ / Auditor-General / Police) covers the three statutory escalation paths Ghanaian whistleblowing law contemplates. See Chapter 44.
- **Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) §39 — sensitive personal data.** All free-text disclosure content (`description`, `desired_outcome`, `incident_location`, `submitter_contact`, `closure_summary`, `subject_label`, `role_context`, action `notes`, evidence `caption`, message `body`) is cast `encrypted` and stored as ciphertext keyed by `APP_KEY`. The test suite asserts the ciphertext is not the plaintext on the raw DB row. See Chapter 26 and Chapter 44.
- **Data Protection Act §40 — retention & erasure.** Reports use `SoftDeletes` and are retained indefinitely in the MVP. A defined retention schedule keyed to case status (e.g. closed-unsubstantiated → purge after N years) is roadmapped. See Chapter 26.
- **ISO 37002 — Whistleblowing management systems.** The module is structured around the four ISO-37002 phases: **receiving** (public form with anonymity default), **assessing** (triage with severity + assignment), **addressing** (action timeline + thread + referral types), and **concluding** (closure with substantiated/unsubstantiated/referred outcomes plus a submitter-visible summary). The 2FA-fresh gate on triage and assign maps to ISO-37002's "secure handling of reports". See Chapter 44.

## What's planned next

The gap analysis (eight days old at the time of writing) marked the Whistleblower module as a Phase 2 priority; the present chapter documents the surface shipped in PR-and-merge cycle around 2026-05-29. Five concrete next steps are on the immediate roadmap:

1. **Evidence download surface.** Files are stored, listed, and metadata is shown — but the controlled-download endpoint (with policy gate, audit-action emission, and short-TTL signed URL) is not yet wired in the investigator UI. This is the single largest functional gap on the admin side and is item one on the Phase 2 follow-up list.
2. **Anonymity hardening — log scrubbing & egress controls.** Today the standard Laravel request log, error log, and APM trace will capture client IP and user-agent for `/whistleblower` POSTs the same way they do for any route. A dedicated middleware that strips the request from the audit/log pipeline for the public whistleblower routes, plus a CAPTCHA (hCaptcha or Ghana-specific alternative) on the submission form, are the two paired changes. The 6/min throttle remains as the floor.
3. **Submitter-side related-case linking.** Today a submitter who returns with new evidence on a closed case must open a fresh, unlinked case. A "this is related to WB-2026-NNNNN" lane on the submission form, plus a `parent_report_id` column, would let an investigator see the family of cases without breaking the one-way-closure rule.
4. **Read-receipt wiring.** The `whistleblower_messages.read_at` column exists; nothing currently writes to it. The next iteration sets it on the investigator's first view of an inbound message, and on the submitter's status-page load for outbound messages.
5. **Defined retention schedule (DPA §40).** The MVP retains everything via soft-delete forever. The Phase 2 retention work brings each case status under a defined window — typically multiple years for substantiated cases, shorter for unsubstantiated — with a quiet, audit-logged purge job. The CHRAJ referral path adds a "transfer of custody" hook that lets the institute drop its retention once CHRAJ accepts the file, so the case content lives in the regulator's system rather than ours.
