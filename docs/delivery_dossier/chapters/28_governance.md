# Chapter 28 — Governance (Policy Register, Acknowledgements, Certifications, Incident Reports)

> *In one paragraph.* Governance is the lane CIHRMS uses for the institute-wide internal-control surface — the **policy register** (HR, finance, IT, compliance, safety, conduct), the **acknowledgement ledger** that proves every member of staff has read each published policy, the **certifications register** that tracks the professional credentials staff hold and warns before they lapse, and the **incident-reports** thread where any employee can lodge a grievance, an improvement suggestion, or a workplace-safety concern into a private "circle" of reviewers. It is intentionally narrower than the legally-protected channel in Chapter 27 — Whistleblower is for statute-grade allegations of corruption, fraud, harassment and the like; this module is the everyday governance shelf. The four sub-surfaces share one Vue page tree (`resources/js/Pages/Governance/...`), one controller pair (`GovernanceController` + `IncidentReportController`), and one permission group (`governance.*` plus the single `incidents.review` permission), but they map to four distinct migration files and four distinct model trees underneath.

## Where to find it

- **Sidebar location:** **Governance** group (building-with-columns icon, deep-indigo `#3949ab` masthead accent). Four child items:
    - **Overview** (`governance.index`) — landing register: hero banner, KPI tiles, your-compliance ring, category-bars panel, policy cards.
    - **Manage** (`governance.manage`) — only rendered when the viewer holds `governance.manage`; the lifecycle workspace for drafting, versioning, and publishing.
    - **Certifications** (`governance.certifications.index`) — the cross-organisation certifications register with the expiry-reminder controls.
    - **Incident Reports** (`incidents.index`) — the private grievance / suggestion / safety thread tree.
  The "Compose Incident" tile is also pinned in the Quick Actions menu and deep-links into `/governance/incidents?new=1` which auto-opens the New Report panel.
- **Roles that see it:**
    - **super_admin** and **ceo** — every surface, every field (super-admin via the `before()` shortcut in `GovernancePolicy`; ceo via the wildcard role grant).
    - **hr_admin** — the canonical policy owner: holds `governance.view`, `governance.manage`, `governance.acknowledge`, **and** `governance.cert_manage`. They draft, version, publish, retire policies and run the certifications register.
    - **manager / dept_head / employee / finance_officer / it_support / auditor** — all hold `governance.view` and `governance.acknowledge`. They see the register, read every published policy, and sign acknowledgement against any policy they have not yet acknowledged. They do **not** see the Manage page (the sidebar entry is hidden, and the controller route is middleware-gated on `governance.manage`).
    - **incidents.review holders** — by seeder design, **no role gets this permission by default**. The intent (`IncidentPermissionsSeeder` comment) is that super-admin or hr-admin grants it explicitly via the RBAC UI to the CEO plus chosen executives — typically the head of HR, the company secretary, and one independent non-executive. Only holders of this permission appear in the "Assign reviewer" picker on an incident.
- **Related modules:** **Whistleblower (Ch 27)** — the statute-grade lane for corruption / fraud / Act 720 disclosures; this chapter's Incident Reports lane is the everyday companion. **Audit Logs (Ch 24)** — every mutation on this page (policy create/update/publish, certification add, incident assign/close) flows through the global Laravel audit pipeline. **DPA (Ch 26)** — `signed_full_name`, `ip_address` and `user_agent` captured on every acknowledgement count as personal data and are kept under the same retention rules as the wider audit chain. **Reports & Analytics (Ch 31)** — every Governance event (`PolicyPublished`, `PolicyAcknowledged`, `CertificationExpiring`, `CertificationExpired`) is registered against `RecordAnalyticsEvent` in `AppServiceProvider`, so the analytics dashboard counts them automatically. **Standards (Ch 44)** — the King IV / ISO 31000 / ISO 37001 mapping for this module lives in the standards index.

There is no N+1 on the cards. The compliance percentage is computed as `(published_count - pending_for_me) / published_count` — when no policies are published the rate displays as 100% rather than divide-by-zero. The editorial masthead's edition number is purely cosmetic: it's derived live from `new Date()`, not stored.

## The data behind it

The Governance module spans seven tables across three feature areas:

- **Policy register** — three tables created in one migration (`2026_05_30_000001_create_policies_tables`):
    - `policies` — title (200), unique slug (200), `category` enum (HR, Finance, IT, Compliance, Safety, Conduct, Other), free-text `summary`, `owner_user_id` FK to the policy steward, `is_active` flag, `current_version_id` self-deferred FK to the published version, soft-deletes. Indexed on `category` and `is_active`.
    - `policy_versions` — every revision of every policy. Carries `version_number`, `body` (longtext markdown), `effective_from` / `effective_to` (date), free-text `changelog`, and the audit pair `published_by` / `published_at`. Unique key on `(policy_id, version_number)`.
    - `policy_acknowledgements` — the per-user, per-version ledger of who signed what. Carries `acknowledged_at`, `ip_address` (45 chars — IPv6-safe), `user_agent` (text), and `signed_full_name` (120). Unique key on `(policy_version_id, user_id)`.
- **Certifications register** — one table (`certifications`, created in `2026_05_20_000003_create_certifications_table` and extended in `2026_05_30_000002_add_reminder_sent_at_to_certifications`):
    - `certifications` — `employee_id` FK, optional `course_id` FK (back-link to the Learning module's course catalogue when the cert came from an internal course completion), `name`, `issuer`, `credential_id`, `issued_at`, `expires_at`, optional `document_path` for an uploaded scan of the certificate, optional `verification_url`, soft-deletes. Added column `reminder_sent_at` (timestamp, indexed) — the one-shot stamp the expiry pipeline writes so a row is not reminded twice.
- **Incident reports** — four tables in one migration (`2026_05_18_000001_create_incident_reports_tables`):
    - `incident_reports` — `employee_id` FK to the submitter, `category` enum (grievance / improvement / safety / other), `title` (180), text `body`, `status` enum (open / in_review / closed) defaulting to open, `closed_at` / `closed_by_id` / `resolution_note` triplet that's all-NULL on open cases. Soft-deletes. Indexes on `(employee_id, status)` and `(status, created_at)`.
    - `incident_report_assignees` — pivot between `incident_reports` and `users`. Carries `assigned_at`, `assigned_by_id`, and a nullable `removed_at`. Primary key is the pair `(incident_report_id, user_id)` — re-assignment after removal updates the existing row rather than inserting a duplicate.
    - `incident_report_messages` — the thread. `author_id` FK to `users`, body text, indexed on `(incident_report_id, created_at)` for the per-case timeline scan.
    - `incident_report_attachments` — polymorphic on `(attachable_type, attachable_id)` so an attachment can hang off either the report itself or any of its messages. Carries `file_path`, `original_name`, `mime_type`, `size_bytes`, `uploaded_by_id`.

What every reader of the screen needs to keep in mind:

- **Acknowledgement is per-version, not per-policy.** Publishing v2 of a policy that everyone signed at v1 immediately resets every staff member's `my_ack_status` for that policy back to `pending`. The Service computes `pendingAcksFor($user)` by checking the existence of a row on `policy_acknowledgements` keyed to the *current* version id only — the trail of older signatures stays in the database, but only the current version counts towards "compliant".
- **Published versions are append-only.** Once a `policy_versions` row has `published_at` set, the publish service refuses to publish it again (`DomainException: "Version N is already published."`). The way to correct a published version is to add a new version, not to mutate the published one. Publishing the new version stamps `effective_to = effective_from - 1 day` on the outgoing version so the historical record has a clean handover date.
- **Incident-report visibility is server-side circle membership, not a role check.** A super-admin who has not been assigned to an incident cannot read it — the `IncidentReportPolicy` deliberately does not implement a `before()` shortcut. The "circle" is the union of the submitter and the *currently-active* assignees, and removing an assignee removes their access in the same request.
- **The signed name must match the account name.** `AcknowledgePolicyRequest::passedValidation()` lowercases and trims both strings and aborts the request with HTTP 422 on mismatch. This is the application-layer "intent to sign" guard — it stops a user clicking through an acknowledgement with a placeholder string.
- **Reminders are one-shot per cert.** `reminder_sent_at` is set the first time a cert enters the 30-day window. A new reminder for the same cert is not sent automatically; the operator triggers a fresh sweep via "Send Reminders Now" on the certifications page (or by re-running the `governance:certification-reminders` Artisan command) only after manually clearing the timestamp, which is a known limitation called out below.

## How it talks to other modules

- **`PolicyDrafted` / `PolicyVersionAdded` / `PolicyPublished` / `PolicyAcknowledged` events** → dispatched at the end of each lifecycle transition. All four are registered in `AppServiceProvider` against `RecordAnalyticsEvent` so they roll up into the Reports & Analytics module's compliance KPIs (Ch 31). No notification listeners are wired in the MVP — the events are the hook points for a future "your policy ack rate is below threshold" digest.
- **`CertificationExpiring` / `CertificationExpired` events** → dispatched by `GovernanceService::dispatchExpiryReminders($daysAhead)` (called from the controller's "Send Reminders Now" button and from the `DispatchCertificationReminders` Artisan command). Both events are registered against `RecordAnalyticsEvent` so the dashboards count them. No notification listener is wired in the MVP — the lapsed-cert email/SMS path is on the Phase 1 roadmap; today the operator sees lapses on the register page itself.
- **Incident-report listener fleet** → five auto-discovered listeners live under `app/Listeners/Incident/`: `NotifyAssignee` (writes a `Notification` row when a reviewer joins the circle), `NotifyUnassigned` (when they leave), `NotifyMessageRecipients` (when a thread reply lands), `NotifySubmitterOnClose` (when the case is closed), and `NotifyCircleOnReopen` (when it returns to in-review). These are wired through Laravel 11's typed-parameter listener auto-discovery, not through explicit `Event::listen` in the provider — registering them twice is a known footgun the provider's source comment flags out loud.
- **Employees (Ch 3)** → every incident report joins via `employee_id` to the submitter's Employee row. The "submitter has no employee profile" failure mode is caught in `IncidentReportService::create()` with a ValidationException, so a staff account that exists without an employee row (a rare data-migration artefact) gets a clean "you must have an employee profile to submit an incident report" error rather than a 500.
- **Learning (Ch 7) — bidirectional certifications link** → completing an internal course that has a `confers_certificate` config emits a `CertificationIssued` event that the Learning module's listener turns into a `certifications` row pinned by `course_id`. From the certifications register's point of view such rows look identical to manually-entered ones; from the Learning module's point of view they are how a course completion becomes a tracked credential.
- **Whistleblower (Ch 27)** → the two modules deliberately share no data and almost no permissions. Incident Reports is for ordinary workplace concerns where the submitter is willing to be named; Whistleblower is the Act-720-grade lane with one-way tracking-code anonymity. The sidebar groups them apart (Governance vs System) and the seeders never grant `whistleblower.*` permissions to anyone who automatically gets `incidents.review`.
- **DPA (Ch 26)** → policy acknowledgements record `ip_address`, `user_agent`, and the typed `signed_full_name` — all three count as personal data under the DPA Act. The retention rule today is "kept indefinitely while the user account exists" via the cascading `policy_acknowledgements.user_id` FK. A defined retention schedule for ex-employee acknowledgements is a Phase 2 follow-up.
- **Audit Logs (Ch 24)** → the global Laravel audit chain captures every authenticated mutation on this module (policy create/update/publish, ack post, certification add/update/delete, incident assign/unassign/post/close/reopen). The incident messaging thread is *not* mirrored into the global chain row-by-row — the per-report `incident_report_messages` table is the per-case audit timeline by design.

## Standards touchpoints

- **King IV Principle 5 — Governance of risk** (also paralleled in the Ghana Code of Best Practice for Public Sector). The Governance module is the operational expression of the principle's "policy framework" requirement: a versioned policy register, owner accountability (`owner_user_id`), a documented publication date (`effective_from`), and proof-of-knowledge (the acknowledgement ledger). What is **not** yet shipped in the MVP and remains explicitly mapped to this principle in the roadmap is the **risk register** itself (a top-N risks table with owner, likelihood, impact, treatment, status) — see "Honest gaps" below. See Chapter 44.
- **King IV Principle 13 — Compliance.** The acknowledgement ledger is the audit-grade evidence of policy reading — typed signature, captured IP, captured user agent, immutable timestamp. The `signed_full_name` match against the account name is the application-layer integrity check. See Chapter 44.
- **ISO 31000 — Risk management framework.** The four-state policy lifecycle (draft → published → effective → archived) maps to ISO 31000's "establish, implement, monitor, review" loop, but only the policy half — the **risk identification + treatment side of the framework is not built in the MVP** and is the single biggest gap against this standard. See Chapter 44 and "Honest gaps".
- **ISO 37001 — Anti-bribery management systems.** The `Conduct` category is the home for the code-of-conduct policy and any anti-bribery policy the institute publishes; the typed-signature acknowledgement of those policies is the evidence ISO 37001 §7.3 (awareness) requires. Anti-bribery-specific case routing is **not** built — those concerns route through the Whistleblower channel (Ch 27), not this module. See Chapter 44.
- **Public Financial Management Act, 2016 (Act 921) — internal controls.** The `Finance` policy category is where the institute publishes its procurement policy, the budgetary-control policy, the expenditure-authorisation policy, and the related internal-control suite the Act expects. The acknowledgement ledger then proves every finance officer has read the current version. The financial-control surface that **enforces** those policies (segregation of duties, approval thresholds, dual control) lives in the Finance module (Ch F1–F5), not here. See Chapter 44.
- **Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) §17–18 — lawful basis & data minimisation.** The `Compliance` category is where the institute's data-protection policy is published; the `signed_full_name` / `ip_address` / `user_agent` captured on acknowledgement are personal-data fields processed under the employment-contract basis. See Chapter 26 and Chapter 44.
- **Labour Act, 2003 (Act 651) §63 — grievance procedure.** The Incident Reports lane is the everyday grievance channel the Labour Act anticipates — a submitter raises a `grievance` category report, the case is assigned to a reviewer, a private thread documents the back-and-forth, and the closure note records the outcome. The reviewers (assignees) are deliberately not bound to be HR — any user the institute grants `incidents.review` can be a reviewer, which lets a small institute give the role to a respected non-HR senior colleague where the grievance is *about* HR. See Chapter 44.

## Honest gaps

The Governance module today is deliberately scoped to the policy + acknowledgement + certification + incident-thread surface that PR-and-merge cycle around 2026-05-30 shipped. The chapter is intentionally short because the module is intentionally short. Five concrete gaps are open against the standards mapping above:

1. **No risk register.** The "risk register" sub-page King IV and ISO 31000 both call for — a table of top organisational risks with owner, likelihood, impact, treatment, residual, status, and review cycle — is **not built** in the MVP. The current Governance module covers policies, not risks. A `risks` table and a "Risk Register" sidebar entry are first-class items on the Phase 2 roadmap; until then the institute records its risk register out-of-band (typically in the board pack).
2. **No board-pack assembly surface.** There is no "Board Pack" button that bundles the current policy versions, the open-incident summary, the certification-expiry list, and the (eventual) risk register into one PDF for board distribution. Board members today receive these surfaces individually. The pack assembly is Phase 2 work and depends on item 1 above landing first.
3. **No committee meeting tracker.** There is no `committees` / `committee_meetings` / `agenda_items` data model. The institute schedules committee meetings out-of-band (calendar invites, shared minutes documents). Building a meeting tracker + minute-publication flow is Phase 2 — the Incident Reports thread is structurally similar enough that it could be the seed for it.
4. **Reminders are one-shot per cert, not a cadence.** `reminder_sent_at` is set the first time a certification enters the 30-day window. The system does not currently send a 7-day-out or 1-day-out follow-up. Implementing a multi-tier cadence (30/7/1) plus a post-expiry "your cert lapsed" alert is a small Phase 1 add — the table already has all the fields it needs.
5. **No expiry notifications wired beyond analytics.** `CertificationExpiring` and `CertificationExpired` events are dispatched and the analytics dashboard counts them, but no email/SMS listener turns them into a personal "your cert is lapsing" message. Today the operator sees lapses on the register page or via the manual "Send Reminders Now" button; the staff member sees nothing automatic. Wiring a notification listener is a one-class change and is on the Phase 1 list.

## What's planned next

Phase 1 of the governance build-out (4–6 weeks, planned for the cycle following the 2026-05-30 PR) lands four things directly on this module:

1. **Risk register table + page.** A new `risks` table (id, title, description, owner_user_id, likelihood enum, impact enum, residual rating, treatment text, status enum, next-review date), a `/governance/risks` register page that mirrors the policy-register layout (KPI tiles, severity-grid panel, filter strip, card grid), and a "Risk owner sign-off" acknowledgement flow modelled on the policy-acknowledgement ledger.
2. **Multi-tier certification reminder cadence.** Extend `DispatchCertificationReminders` to honour a configurable cadence (30/7/1 by default), persist the cadence stamps to a `certification_reminders` child table rather than a single column, and wire the `CertificationExpiring` / `CertificationExpired` events to a `Notification` listener so the certificate holder is told personally — not just the operator.
3. **Markdown rendering upgrade.** Replace the lightweight inline renderer in `Show.vue` with a server-side parse to sanitised HTML (Laravel `commonmark` package) so policy authors can use tables, blockquotes, ordered lists, and code blocks. Today's renderer covers headings, bullets and paragraphs only.
4. **Board-pack assembler v0.** A single "Generate Board Pack" button on the Governance overview that produces a PDF bundle of: the current policy register snapshot, the certifications-expiring-next-90-days table, the open-incident-reports summary (counts only — no body), and a placeholder section for the risk register (filled in once item 1 above lands). The Auditor-General report pack (Ch 24) is the template; the board pack is a friendlier-shaped cousin.

Phase 2 adds the committee meeting tracker (item 3 in the gaps list), the formal retention schedule for ex-employee acknowledgements (item from the DPA cross-reference), and an "Anti-bribery declaration" recurring acknowledgement flow tied to the ISO 37001 §7.3 awareness requirement — a once-a-year forced re-sign on the code-of-conduct policy, with the cadence enforced server-side rather than relying on HR to re-publish the policy.
