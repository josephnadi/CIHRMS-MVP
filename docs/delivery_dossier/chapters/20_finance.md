# Chapter 20 — Finance (Chart of Accounts, AP, AR, Paystack Gateway, Bank Reconciliation)

> *In one paragraph.* Finance is the double-entry general ledger that sits underneath every cedi the institute owes, holds, receives, or pays. It is built in five tightly-coupled sub-modules — F1 *Chart of Accounts + Org Bank Accounts + Finance Hub* (the schema and the dashboard), F2 *Accounts Payable* (vendor invoices, payments, and the journal-posting engine that everything else routes through), F3 *Accounts Receivable* (customer invoices, receipts, statements, write-offs), F4 *Paystack hosted-checkout gateway* (customer self-service card and MoMo payments with signed webhooks), and F5 *Bank Reconciliation* (CSV / OFX 2.x / MT940 SWIFT statement imports with a three-tier auto-matcher and bank-adjustment journals). Every business event — a vendor invoice's accrual, an AR receipt's allocation, a Paystack webhook's auto-receipt, a bank-fee adjustment — lands as a balanced journal entry on the same `journal_entries` / `journal_lines` pair, and every one of those entries flows through `JournalPostingService::post()`, the only piece of code in the system permitted to mutate `gl_account_balances`. That single invariant — *debits equal credits at every posting, gl-balances reflect the natural sum of posted lines* — is what makes the Finance Hub's cash position, the customer statement's running balance, and the bank reconciliation's percentage all reconcilable against the same audit trail.

## Where to find it

- **Sidebar location:** **Finance** group → **Finance Hub** (top-level item, scales icon), and the five sub-module links underneath: **Chart of Accounts**, **Bank Accounts**, **Vendors**, **AP Invoices**, **AP Payments**, **Journal**, **Customers**, **AR Invoices**, **AR Receipts**, **Customer Statements**, **Payment Links**, **Bank Reconciliation**. The Hub tile is also pinned in the app switcher (the 3×3 grid in the top bar) for any user with `finance.hub`. The Finance group is the only sidebar group whose every entry is RBAC-gated at the route layer rather than just the policy layer — a user without the matching permission slug never gets the entry rendered.
- **Roles that see it:**
    - **super_admin** and **ceo** — every screen, every field, every action including refunds (gateway.refund is reserved for the super_admin wildcard).
    - **finance_officer** — the operational owner. Holds all `accounts.*`, `bank_accounts.*`, `vendors.*`, `customers.*`, `ap_invoices.*`, `ap_payments.*`, `ar_invoices.*`, `ar_receipts.*`, `journal.view`, `journal.post_manual`, `gateway.view`, `gateway.create`, `statements.view`, `reconciliation.view`, `reconciliation.import`, `reconciliation.match`, `reconciliation.adjust`, `finance.hub`. Approval of any invoice the same officer created is refused at the service layer (dual control).
    - **hr_admin** — sees the Finance Hub (it shares the payroll-readiness band) and the chart of accounts read-only; cannot post journals, record payments, or import statements.
    - **auditor** — read-only across every Finance screen including the journal explorer, statutory compliance band, and the reconciliation history. Cannot link, unlink, post, refund, or import.
    - **dept_head / manager / employee** — do not reach `/finance` directly. They see only the AR side of their own profile (chargebacks for training, certifications, dues) through the Profile portal (Chapter 16) and any payment links sent to them by Finance.
    - **it_support** — no Finance permissions by default.
- **Related modules:** Payroll Engine (Ch 19) — every approved payroll run mints a balanced GIFMIS journal voucher that lands in the same `journal_entries` table once the GIFMIS exporter promotes from "download" to "auto-mint on paid"; for now the JV file is downloadable from the run, the Finance Hub surfaces it under Statutory Compliance, and the disbursement-side cash movement posts through `ApPaymentService` once Finance issues the GhIPSS batch. Payments (Ch 18) — one-off payslip-style payments not tied to a payroll run land as an `ApPayment` with an `ad_hoc` narration. Disbursements (Ch 22) — Materialised disbursements from a payroll run carry the E-Levy split (1.5% on MoMo channels per Act 1075) and reference an `ApPayment` when they clear; Paystack also appears in Ch 22's outbound rail catalogue alongside Zoho. Loans (Ch 21) — loan disbursement debits a loan-receivable GL and credits the source bank GL through a manual JE; monthly repayments collected by the payroll engine post against the same loan-receivable GL via the journal-posting engine. Audit Logs (Ch 24) — every journal entry, every reversal, every payment intent state change, every webhook delivery, every reconciliation link and unlink is event-sourced. Standards & Statute Index (Ch 44) — IFRS double-entry, GIFMIS sub-ledger JV format, CAGD chart-of-accounts mapping, Bank of Ghana transaction reporting, DPA Act 843 (for vendor/customer individuals), PFM Act 921 (financial controls), and Electronic Transfer Levy Act 1075 (E-Levy) all anchor here. Roadmap (Ch 46) — Phase 1 promotes Paystack refunds to operator UI behind a fresh-2FA gate, wires the auto-expiry cron for stale payment intents, replaces the count-based reference fallback with the row-locked `SequenceService` everywhere it isn't yet, and adds the printable bank-rec audit pack.

## Every button, every action

This chapter has **five** sub-sections — one per F-area. F1 is the dashboard and the foundation; F2 introduces the journal engine and uses it for the AP outflow; F3 mirrors the pattern for AR; F4 adds the customer-pays-online path that routes through F3; F5 closes the loop by matching the bank's record of reality against what F2-F4 say happened.

> *Notes:* `FinanceHubService::summaryFor()` caches the whole response per-user for 60 seconds — long enough to absorb a refresh storm during morning stand-up, short enough that an approval flow finishes inside the same minute. The cash-position query is the only one that does an `org_bank_accounts ⋈ gl_accounts ⋈ gl_account_balances` join; everything else aggregates a single table. Aging buckets use `Carbon::diffInDays(absolute: true)` and a PHP-side bucket walk because SQLite + Postgres date arithmetic diverges enough that a single SQL expression isn't worth the maintenance cost over a typically-small outstanding set.

The unique constraint on the reference column is what stops a duplicate from ever landing.

> *Notes on the engine.* `JournalPostingService::post(JournalEntry $entry)` is the only code path that mutates `gl_account_balances`. The invariant: every business event mints a `JournalEntry` with `status = draft`, attaches `JournalLine` rows (debit_amount XOR credit_amount per line — never both, never neither for a non-zero line), and `post()` validates that (a) the entry is in draft, (b) it has at least two lines, (c) `isBalanced()` returns true (`sum(debit_amount) == sum(credit_amount)` to two decimals). Then inside a `DB::transaction`: walks every line, computes the natural delta against the line's GL type, `lockForUpdate()`s the matching `gl_account_balances` row, adds the delta, stamps `last_posted_at`, saves; finally flips the JE to `Posted`, stamps `posted_at` / `posted_by`, dispatches `JournalEntryPosted`. A missing balance row is a domain error, not a silent zero-balance create. Reversal mints a new JE with debits and credits swapped, posts it through the same `post()` (so the reversal is a real business transaction, not a magic balance correction), then marks the **original** as Reversed; the reversal itself stays Posted. This is what allows the balance-invariant query to read `whereIn('status', [Posted, Reversed])` and still see every line that ever contributed to a balance.

#### The webhook path

| Surface | What it does | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|
| **`POST /webhooks/paystack`** | Three-line controller — extracts `data.id` from the payload, INSERTs a `paystack_webhook_events` row keyed on `paystack_event_id` (UNIQUE — replayed deliveries collide on the index and the controller short-circuits to a 200), dispatches `ProcessPaystackWebhook`. Always returns 200 so Paystack stops retrying. | The idempotency guard is at the storage layer, not in application logic — the database is the authority. |
| **`VerifyPaystackSignature` middleware** | `hash_hmac('sha512', $request->getContent(), $secret)` compared with `hash_equals()` for constant-time check. Reads the raw body via `getContent()` so it must run BEFORE Laravel parses JSON — applied at the route alias, not in a global stack. | Anyone can post to a public webhook URL; the signature is the only thing that says it's Paystack. |
| **`ProcessPaystackWebhook` queued job** | Fetches the event row, delegates to `PaystackWebhookProcessor::process()`. `tries = 3`, `backoff = 30s`. | Webhooks should never run in the request thread — Paystack's retry budget is finite, ours is unlimited inside the queue. |
| **`PaystackWebhookProcessor::process()`** | Short-circuits on `processed_at != null` (true idempotency). Dispatches on `event_type` — `charge.success` runs `handleChargeSuccess`, `refund.processed` runs `handleRefundProcessed`, anything else marks no-op. | Two event types matter; everything else is logged but ignored to keep behaviour predictable. |
| **`handleChargeSuccess`** | Locks the `payment_intents` row with `lockForUpdate()`; idempotently returns the existing receipt if status is already Success; refuses to post if status is anything other than Pending; calls `gateway->verifyTransaction()` against Paystack's `/transaction/verify/{ref}` as a belt-and-braces check against signature-key compromise; refuses on amount mismatch (expected pesewas vs. reported pesewas); resolves the receiving bank by `OrgBankAccount::forPurpose($paystack.receipt_bank_purpose)->active()`; routes the receipt through `ArReceiptService::record()` with `external_ref = paystack_reference` (F5's Tier-1 matcher); updates the intent to Success, stamps `paid_at` / `ar_receipt_id`; closes the event with `processed_at`. | Every check has a single failure mode that writes to `processing_error` rather than crash — a finance officer can read exactly *why* a webhook didn't post a receipt. |

> *Notes on what doesn't happen.* `PaystackWebhookProcessor` does **not** re-verify against Paystack from anywhere downstream — F5's reconciliation does not re-verify either. Once `external_ref` is populated on the receipt row, the column is the truth. Any future inbound gateway (Stripe, Flutterwave, an alternative MoMo provider) is expected to follow the same shape: store-layer idempotency UNIQUE, signature middleware, queued processor, delegation to `ArReceiptService::record()`. Never write to `ar_receipts` directly from a webhook.

#### Three-tier matching — how it decides

| Tier | Confidence | Test | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | **high** | Exact `external_ref` match on the same bank account, or the AP/AR `external_ref` appears as a substring in the statement-line `description`. | Auto-link if exactly one candidate. |
| **Tier 2** | **medium** | Same bank account, amount equal within 0.005, transaction date within ±2 days, **and** the CIHRMS record's `reference` appears as a substring in the line description. | Auto-link if exactly one candidate. |
| **Tier 3** | **low** | Same bank account, amount equal within 0.005, transaction date within ±2 days. | Single candidate → auto-link with low confidence; multiple → leave unmatched but stamp `confidence = low` so the UI surfaces the candidate set; zero → unmatched. |

> *Notes on the boundary.* `JournalPostingService` is **unmodified** by F5 — verified at PR time by `git diff feat/finance-f4-paystack feat/finance-f5-bank-reconciliation -- app/Services/Finance/JournalPostingService.php` being empty. `ArReceiptService::record()` is also unmodified — F5 reads receipt rows but never writes them. The polymorphic `matched_type` on `bank_statement_lines` is a plain string column rather than a real Eloquent morph_type with a DB-level FK because three different targets (`ApPayment`, `ArReceipt`, `JournalEntry`) cannot share a single FK; the service code is solely responsible for never writing an invalid class string. `bank_transaction_matches` is append-only — unlink stamps `unmatched_at` rather than deleting; the immutable history is what lets the auditor reconstruct who linked and unlinked what, and when.

## The data behind it

CIHRMS stores the Finance ledger across **fifteen** tables. The chart of accounts and bank-account catalogue are the foundation; the journal pair is the engine; the AP and AR tables are the operational ledgers; the Paystack and reconciliation tables are the gateway and the bank-side audit trail.

### Chart of accounts and balances

- **`gl_accounts`** — one row per GL code. Carries the code (`unique`, includes soft-deleted rows for audit-trail continuity), name, type (`GlAccountType`: Asset / Liability / Equity / Income / Expense), optional `parent_id` for the hierarchy, `is_active` flag, description, soft-delete.
- **`gl_account_balances`** — read-side cache, 1:1 with `gl_accounts`. PK = `gl_account_id` (no surrogate id, cascadeOnDelete). Holds `balance` (natural sign — positive when the account is on its expected side) and `last_posted_at`. **Sole mutator: `JournalPostingService::post()`.** Direct writes from anywhere else are a bug — the invariant `balance == natural-sum-of-posted-journal-lines` is what every report rests on.
- **`org_bank_accounts`** — the institute's own bank accounts. Each row carries the linked `gl_account_id` (asset GL only), bank name, branch, account name, number, sort code, SWIFT, currency (GHS default), `purpose` (`OrgBankAccountPurpose`: Operating / Payroll / Statutory Escrow / Receipts / Reserve), opening balance, active flag, notes, soft-delete.

### The journal engine

- **`journal_entries`** — one row per double-entry event. Carries the human-readable `reference` (`JE-YYYY-NNNNNN` minted by `SequenceService::next('journal:<year>')`; reversal references use the `JR-` prefix and `journal_reversal:<year>` key), `entry_date`, narration, status (`JournalEntryStatus`: Draft / Posted / Reversed), `source_type` (`JournalSourceType`: Manual / VendorInvoice / ApPayment / ArInvoice / ArReceipt / BankAdjustment), polymorphic `source_id` pointing at the originating row, `reversal_of_id` when this entry is a reversal, audit columns (`created_by`, `posted_at`, `posted_by`, `reversed_at`, `reversed_by`).
- **`journal_lines`** — one row per debit or credit. Carries `journal_entry_id`, `line_no` (1..n in display order), `gl_account_id`, `debit_amount`, `credit_amount` (exactly one of debit/credit is non-zero per line for a non-zero amount), per-line narration. The balance invariant is enforced at posting time by `JournalEntry::isBalanced()` — `sum(debit_amount) == sum(credit_amount)` to two decimals.

### Accounts Payable

- **`vendors`** — code (unique), name, tax id, status (`VendorStatus`: Active / Inactive / Suspended), contact details, three default-GL columns (`default_expense_gl_account_id`, `default_ap_gl_account_id`, `default_bank_account_id`), notes, soft-delete.
- **`vendor_invoices`** — reference (`API-YYYY-NNNNNN`, unique), `vendor_id`, optional `vendor_invoice_no` (per-vendor unique when present), invoice date, due date, status (`VendorInvoiceStatus`: Draft / PendingApproval / Approved / PartiallyPaid / Paid / Cancelled), subtotal, tax_amount, total, `amount_paid`, currency, notes, `accrual_journal_entry_id` (FK to the JE that posted the accrual on approval), `cancellation_journal_entry_id`, audit columns. Soft-delete.
- **`vendor_invoice_lines`** — line_no, description, quantity, unit price, tax rate, line total, tax amount, `gl_account_id` (expense GL).
- **`ap_payments`** — reference (`APP-YYYY-NNNNNN`, unique), `vendor_id`, payment date, amount, currency, `org_bank_account_id`, narration, status (`ApPaymentStatus`: Pending / Processed / Voided), `external_ref` (nullable, indexed — F5 Tier-1), `payment_journal_entry_id`, `void_journal_entry_id`, audit columns.
- **`ap_payment_invoice_allocations`** — `ap_payment_id`, `vendor_invoice_id`, `allocated_amount` — the many-to-many between payments and invoices.

### Accounts Receivable

- **`customers`** — code (unique), name, tax id, status (`CustomerStatus`: Active / Inactive / Suspended), contact, three default-GL columns (`default_income_gl_account_id`, `default_ar_gl_account_id`, `default_bank_account_id`), notes, soft-delete.
- **`ar_invoices`** — reference (`ARI-YYYY-NNNNNN`, unique), `customer_id`, optional `customer_invoice_no` (per-customer unique when present), invoice date, due date, status (`ArInvoiceStatus`: Draft / PendingApproval / Approved / PartiallyPaid / Paid / Cancelled / WrittenOff), subtotal, tax_amount, total, `amount_received`, currency, notes, `accrual_journal_entry_id`, `write_off_journal_entry_id`, audit columns. Soft-delete.
- **`ar_invoice_lines`** — same shape as `vendor_invoice_lines` but the GL is an income account.
- **`ar_receipts`** — reference (`ARC-YYYY-NNNNNN`, unique), `customer_id`, receipt date, amount, currency, `org_bank_account_id`, narration, status (`ArReceiptStatus`: Pending / Processed / Voided), **`external_ref`** (nullable, indexed — F4 webhook + F5 Tier-1), `receipt_journal_entry_id`, `void_journal_entry_id`, audit columns.
- **`ar_receipt_invoice_allocations`** — `ar_receipt_id`, `ar_invoice_id`, `allocated_amount`.

### Paystack gateway

- **`payment_intents`** — `reference` (`PI-YYYY-NNNNNN`, unique), `customer_id`, `ar_invoice_id`, amount, currency, status (`PaymentIntentStatus`: Created / Pending / Success / Failed / Abandoned / Expired / Refunded), `paystack_reference` (unique — initialized on first gateway call), `paystack_access_code`, `authorization_url`, `callback_url`, `last_paystack_response` (json), `expires_at` (+24h from initialization), `paid_at`, `ar_receipt_id`, `refund_paystack_ref`, `refund_settled_at`, soft-delete.
- **`paystack_webhook_events`** — append-only audit log of every webhook delivery; `public $timestamps = false`. `paystack_event_id` UNIQUE (the idempotency guard), `event_type`, `paystack_reference`, raw `payload` (json), `received_at`, `processed_at`, `processing_error`, polymorphic links to the resulting `payment_intent_id` and `ar_receipt_id` once known.

### Bank reconciliation

- **`bank_statements`** — `org_bank_account_id`, `statement_date`, `opening_balance`, `closing_balance`, `total_lines`, `reconciled_lines`, `format` (csv/ofx/mt940), `file_hash` (SHA-256, UNIQUE — idempotency guard), `file_path`, `imported_at`, `imported_by`. Soft-delete.
- **`bank_statement_lines`** — `bank_statement_id`, `line_no`, `transaction_date`, **`amount`** (signed — positive = credit, negative = debit), `description`, optional `reference`, polymorphic `matched_type` (string class name: `App\Models\ApPayment` / `App\Models\ArReceipt` / `App\Models\JournalEntry`) + `matched_id`, `confidence` (high/medium/low/manual), `reconciled_at`, `reconciled_by`.
- **`bank_transaction_matches`** — append-only audit log. `bank_statement_line_id`, `matched_type`, `matched_id`, `confidence`, `matched_at`, `matched_by`, `unmatched_at`, `unmatched_by`, `unmatched_reason`. Unlink stamps the unmatched columns rather than deleting the row.

### Reference generation

All Finance human-readable references (`APP-`, `API-`, `ARI-`, `ARC-`, `PI-`, `JE-`, `JR-`) are minted by `App\Services\Finance\SequenceService::next(string $key): int` against a `finance_sequences` table with `DB::transaction` + `lockForUpdate()`. The PR #21 fix replaced the legacy `count() + 1` pattern that could race under concurrent transactions. Existing keys: `app_payment`, `ap_invoice`, `ar_invoice`, `ar_receipt`, `payment_intent`, `journal`, `journal_reversal`. New finance services must inject `SequenceService` and use the same scheme; the migration `2026_06_11_000001_create_finance_sequences.php` seeds `current_value` from the historical MAX so the first call after deploy can never collide.

## The double-entry invariants

Three invariants make the rest of the system trustable. Every Finance audit and every dossier reader's confidence rests on these.

1. **Every journal entry is balanced at posting.** `JournalEntry::isBalanced()` returns true if `sum(debit_amount) == sum(credit_amount)` across its `journal_lines` to two decimals. `JournalPostingService::post()` refuses any entry where this is false, with a `DomainException` that names the residual. No business event in CIHRMS — vendor invoice approval, AP payment record, AR invoice approval, AR receipt record, AR write-off, bank adjustment, manual JE — can post without satisfying this check at the line level. Reversals are constructed by swapping debits and credits on the original, so they are balanced by construction.

2. **Every posted line moves `gl_account_balances` through `JournalPostingService` and nothing else.** The natural-delta formula is `Asset / Expense → debit − credit`; `Liability / Equity / Income → credit − debit`. The balance row is locked with `lockForUpdate()` inside the posting transaction; concurrent postings serialise on the lock. A missing `gl_account_balances` row is a domain error, not a silent zero-balance create — the seeder is expected to populate one row per `gl_accounts` row at provisioning time. Reversal mints a new JE, posts it through the same `post()` (so it really mutates balances), then marks the **original** as Reversed; the reversal itself stays Posted because it is a real business transaction that contributed to the balance. The balance-invariant query is `whereIn('status', [Posted, Reversed])` for this reason.

3. **Every business event's source is reconstructable from its JE.** `journal_entries.source_type` (the `JournalSourceType` enum) plus `source_id` point back at the originating row. Going the other way, every AP/AR/payment-intent row carries a JE-id column for each event it triggered (`accrual_journal_entry_id`, `payment_journal_entry_id`, `void_journal_entry_id`, `write_off_journal_entry_id`, `cancellation_journal_entry_id`). The auditor can follow either direction — JE to source, or source to JE — without crossing into application-level inference.

## How it talks to other modules

- **Payroll Engine (Ch 19)** — the payroll engine's GIFMIS journal voucher (one balanced JV per approved run; debits salary expense + employer SSNIT + employer Tier-2, credits net-pay-payable + PAYE + SSNIT employee/employer split + NHIA + Tier-2 + Tier-3 + voluntary) lands as a downloadable CSV today and as an auto-minted `journal_entries` row in Phase 2's `payroll.gifmis.auto_mint_on_paid = true` path. The exporter refuses to write a file whose debits don't equal credits to two decimals — same invariant the Finance posting engine enforces. The Finance Hub's Statutory Compliance band surfaces the per-kind latest return so the close-of-period dashboard knows whether PAYE / SSNIT / NHIA / Tier-2 schedules have been filed.
- **Payments (Ch 18)** — one-off payslip-style payments not tied to a payroll run land as an `ap_payment` with the `vendor_id` pointing at a synthetic "Internal — Ad-hoc Payee" vendor; the journal is the same Dr AP-liability / Cr cash-at-bank shape.
- **Disbursements (Ch 22)** — payroll disbursements (the `disbursements` table — Chapter 22) materialise from approved payroll runs and carry the E-Levy split (1.5% on MoMo channels per the Electronic Transfer Levy Act 2022 (Act 1075)); when each batch clears, an `ap_payment` is recorded against the payroll-net-pay-payable GL and the corresponding org bank GL. Paystack also appears in Ch 22's outbound rail catalogue alongside Zoho for partner payouts.
- **Loans (Ch 21)** — loan disbursement debits the loan-receivable GL (asset) and credits the funding-bank GL via a manual JE through `journal.post_manual`. Repayments collected by the payroll engine (`LoanService::postRepayment()` called during run approval) flip a portion of the receivable into cash through the same path.
- **Audit Logs (Ch 24)** — `JournalEntryPosted`, `PaymentIntentCreated`, `PaymentIntentSucceeded`, `PaystackWebhookReceived`, `BankStatementImported`, `BankStatementLineLinked`, `BankStatementLineUnlinked`, `BankAdjustmentPosted` all dispatch with the actor identity, the affected reference, the run id where applicable, and the 2FA challenge id when behind `2fa:fresh`. They land in the immutable audit log.
- **Webhooks (Ch 27)** — outbound webhooks for `vendor_invoice.approved`, `ap_payment.recorded`, `ar_invoice.approved`, `ar_receipt.recorded`, `payment_intent.succeeded`, and `bank_statement.imported` are dispatched through the `FanOutWebhooks` listener so a partner MDA's downstream ledger or a Big-Four auditor's continuous-audit pipeline can react in real time.
- **Profile portal (Ch 16)** — customers who are individuals (members, certificate holders, training enrolees) see their own AR side from the Profile portal — open invoices, paid receipts, payment links sent to them, statements they can self-generate.
- **Public API v1 (Ch 28)** — `GET /api/v1/finance/journal/entries`, `/journal/entries/{id}`, `/ap-invoices`, `/ar-invoices`, `/payment-intents`, and `/reconciliation/statements` expose the ledger and the operational tables behind the `api.scope:finance:read` middleware; write endpoints are deliberately not in the MVP API surface — every finance write must go through the Inertia UI or the internal service layer for now.

## Standards touchpoints

Forward references all go to **Chapter 44 — Standards & Statute Index**.

- **IFRS — general principles, IAS 1 (Presentation of Financial Statements), IAS 8 (Accounting Policies, Changes and Errors)** — the journal posting engine implements the double-entry invariant at posting time; the `gl_account_balances` row is the natural-sign sum of every posted line and the balance-invariant query that reconciles the ledger reads `whereIn('status', [Posted, Reversed])` so reversal accounting matches IAS 8's expectation that errors are corrected by retrospective restatement rather than silent edit. The chart of accounts permanently retires codes on archive (the unique index includes soft-deleted rows) to preserve historical comparability. See Chapter 44.
- **IFRS 9 — Financial Instruments / Expected credit loss** — the AR write-off path posts a separate bad-debt-expense JE (Dr 5600 Bad Debt Expense, Cr AR) for the **outstanding residual** rather than reversing the original accrual, so revenue stays recognised and the impairment is recognised in the period the recoverability changed. The aging buckets (current / 1–30 / 31–60 / 61+ overdue against `due_date`) are the inputs an IFRS 9 ECL provisioning model would use; the explicit provisioning model is roadmapped in Phase 2. See Chapter 44.
- **GIFMIS — Ghana Integrated Financial Management Information System sub-ledger JV format** — the payroll engine's `GifmisJournalExporter` emits the pipe-delimited CSV that the GIFMIS bulk-loader accepts (`journal_id|line_no|gl_code|cost_centre|dr_amount|cr_amount|narration|period|source_doc|reference`) and refuses to write a file whose debits don't equal credits to two decimals; the GL code map is per-MDA via env vars under `config/payroll.php` → `gifmis.gl_codes`, so the same exporter serves every Controller & Accountant-General-mapped CAGD chart. Phase 3 promotes this from "download and upload" to a live REST push under `payroll.gifmis.auto_mint_on_paid = true`. See Chapter 19, Chapter 44.
- **CAGD — Controller and Accountant-General's Department chart of accounts** — public-sector MDAs adopting CIHRMS map their existing 5-segment CAGD chart (Fund / Function / Programme / Sub-programme / Source-of-funds + Object Code) onto the system's `gl_accounts.code` field; the env-driven GL code map for GIFMIS export is what carries the cross-reference. The seeded chart ships with the universal CAGD-aligned families (1xxx assets, 2xxx liabilities, 3xxx equity, 4xxx income, 5xxx expense) so a fresh install is operable on day one and gradually replaced by the MDA's authoritative chart through Edit + Archive. See Chapter 44.
- **Bank of Ghana — Foreign Exchange Act 2006 (Act 723) and transaction reporting circulars** — multi-currency posting is explicitly out of scope for the MVP (GHS-only); when Phase 3 introduces FX revaluation, the `currency` column on every Finance table is the hook, and the FX-gain/loss GL pair (4900 / 5900) is already in the seeded chart for the day the FX module turns on.
- **Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843), §§17–18 — Lawful basis and data minimisation for vendor / customer personal data** — when a vendor or customer is an individual (a sole-trader supplier, a member, a trainee), their `name`, `email`, `phone`, `address`, and `tax_id` are personal data under the Act. The Finance Resources do not expose them to viewers without the matching permission; the Profile portal slice for individuals is the same row, gated by `whenLoaded` on the relationships; bulk export (the customer statement print) is behind `statements.view` rather than `customers.view` alone. See Chapter 44.
- **Public Financial Management Act, 2016 (Act 921), §11 — Internal controls and separation of duties** — dual control on every approval is enforced at the service layer (`VendorInvoiceService::approve`, `ArInvoiceService::approve` both throw `DomainException: creator cannot approve own record`) and at the policy layer where applicable. Fresh 2FA is required on every cash-moving endpoint — AP payment store and void, AR receipt store and void, AR write-off, manual JE store, payment intent creation, refund (single and bulk), and bank-adjustment post. The combination satisfies §11's separation-of-duties expectation for any disbursement against the Consolidated Fund. See Chapter 19, Chapter 44.
- **Electronic Transfer Levy Act, 2022 (Act 1075) — E-Levy on MoMo disbursements** — applied at 1.5% in `BatchDisbursementService` (sourced from `statutory_rates.E_LEVY_RATE`, fallback 0.015) on every MoMo-channel disbursement; bank-channel and GhIPSS-channel disbursements are not levied. The levy is computed and recorded against the Disbursement row separately from the net-to-recipient amount so the auditor can see who received less than their gross net because of the levy. See Chapter 22, Chapter 44.
- **Cheque Act, 1961 (Act 70) and Bills of Exchange Act, 1961 (Act 55)** — bank reconciliation's three-tier matcher accepts cheque-line descriptions ("CHQ 100234" / "CHEQUE PAID 100234") via the Tier-2 substring search on `description ⊇ reference`, so a cheque-only operating environment reconciles without operator intervention provided the AP payment's reference matches the issued cheque number.
- **ISO 27001 — webhook signature verification** — `VerifyPaystackSignature` middleware applies `hash_hmac('sha512', $rawBody, $secret)` with constant-time `hash_equals()` comparison; the raw body is read via `getContent()` BEFORE JSON parsing so a JSON-canonicalisation attack cannot fool the verifier. See Chapter 44.
- **ISO 30414 §§4.8 / 4.9 (workforce cost, productivity)** — the AP-outstanding tile, the AR-outstanding aging, and the Cash Position number all feed the institute-wide finance disclosure pack in Chapter 13 (Analytics). The line-level breakdown is what makes the disclosure defensible.

## Honest gaps

- **No operator UI for Paystack refunds outside super_admin.** `gateway.refund` is reserved for the super_admin wildcard in the RBAC seeder; the per-row and bulk refund modals are wired and tested, but the seeder grants the permission only to super_admin in the MVP. Phase 1 adds `gateway.refund` to `finance_officer` behind an explicit grant in the seeder.
- **No automatic intent-expiry cron.** `PaymentIntentService::expireStale()` exists and works (flips Pending intents past `expires_at + grace` to Expired), but is not yet scheduled. The operator filter on the Payment Links page surfaces the same set; Phase 1 wires a nightly `php artisan schedule:command` to call it.
- **No printable bank-rec report binding.** The Reconciliation detail page exposes "View / Print" and "Download PDF" buttons, the routes (`finance.reconciliation.print`) are wired, but the PDF template itself is a placeholder shell — the full audit-ready layout (header, opening balance, line-by-line match table, adjustments summary, closing balance, signature block) is on the F5-era backlog.
- **No bulk operator actions on reconciliation.** "Accept all Tier-3 suggestions" and "Unlink all low-confidence matches" are deliberately out for F5 — auto-linking at low confidence is already the default, so the bulk action is mostly a convenience for unlinking after a corrective import.
- **Manual JE reversal lacks a route.** `JournalPostingService::reverse()` is callable from service code (used by every cancel and void path in AP / AR), but there is no public route to reverse a manually-posted JE without going through the originating subsystem. The Phase 1 ledger-admin pack adds `POST /finance/journal/{entry}/reverse` behind `journal.post_manual` + `2fa:fresh`.
- **AP `external_ref` not auto-populated from GhIPSS.** F5's Tier-1 matcher reads `ap_payments.external_ref` but the column is operator-populated for AP today (manually keyed in, or back-populated when an operator links a bank statement line to an AP payment). The GhIPSS settlement-message bridge that populates it automatically lives in Disbursements' roadmap; until it lands, AP reconciliation is one or two manual links per batch rather than full auto-match.
- **MT940 parser is permissive.** Tested with a synthetic fixture in `tests/Fixtures/Finance/Statements/sample.mt940`; real-world bank exports vary widely in the `:61:` subfield ordering. The first production failure on a real MT940 will sharpen the parser; until then the import-time `StatementParseException` is the only safety net.
- **`ApPaymentService::void()` lacks `lockForUpdate` on the allocation rows.** `record()` correctly locks the target invoices; `void()` does not. The race is between a concurrent record-and-void on the same payment, which is a narrow window; the fix is on the next Finance hardening pass.
- **Multi-currency posting is GHS-only.** Every Finance table carries a `currency` column for the day FX revaluation comes online (Phase 3); for now the FormRequests refuse anything other than GHS at validation, and the cash-position query does not multiply by an exchange rate because there is no FX rate table yet.

## What's planned next

Phase 2 introduces IFRS-grade trial-balance and income-statement report packs (PDF + Excel) on top of the live `gl_account_balances`, an IFRS 9 ECL provisioning model that reads the aging buckets and writes a separate `bad_debt_provision` JE per period-end, and the GIFMIS auto-mint promotion that flips `payroll.gifmis.auto_mint_on_paid` to true for pilot MDAs.
