# Chapter 7 — Learning & Development

> *In one paragraph.* Learning & Development is the institute's catalogue of internal and external courses, the place where every employee can enrol themselves and log their progress, and the place where HR / L&D can publish new courses, watch skills accumulate across departments through a heat-map, and record the external certifications people walk in with. The gap analysis (Chapter 44) classified an LMS as Phase-3 work, on the grounds that government bodies in Ghana usually pay for a separate platform. In practice the team built a leaner version inside CIHRMS during Wave 13: three pages, two tables, one event-driven skill grant, and one nightly job that reminds people their certifications are about to expire. It is not a content-delivery LMS — videos, PDFs, SCORM packages, quizzes, and graded assessments are not stored or played by CIHRMS itself — but it is a working register of who has learned what, what they are working on right now, and what credentials they hold, and that turns out to be the part the institute most needs first.

## Where to find it

- **Sidebar location:** **Learning** group (cyan colour code, "school" icon). Expanding the group reveals three entries — **Catalogue** (the landing page at `/learning`), **My Learning** (`/learning/my`), and **Skills Matrix** (`/learning/skills-matrix`). The group is gated behind `learning.view`; the Skills Matrix entry carries its own `visible: can('learning.manage')` flag so an ordinary employee never sees a dead link to a page they cannot enter.
- **Roles that see it:**
    - **super_admin** and **ceo** — every page, every course (including drafts), every enrolment, every certification.
    - **hr_admin** — every page including the Skills Matrix; `learning.manage` lets them publish, edit, unpublish, and delete courses, issue certifications on behalf of any employee, and add new skills to the institute-wide skill catalogue.
    - **dept_head / manager** — Catalogue and My Learning plus the Skills Matrix (they carry `learning.manage` in the seeded role grants). They can publish a course, but practically the institute reserves that to HR / L&D.
    - **employee** — Catalogue (published courses only) and My Learning. They can enrol themselves, log their own progress, mark themselves complete, and record external certifications against their own employee row. They do not see the Skills Matrix.
    - **finance_officer**, **it_support**, **marketing**, **auditor** — `learning.view` only. They can browse the catalogue and use My Learning; they cannot publish, manage, or open the Skills Matrix.
- **Related modules:** Employees (Ch 3) — every enrolment, certification, and granted skill hangs off exactly one `employee_id`, and the same `EmployeeSkill` rows the Skills Matrix reads from are the ones that render on the Employee detail page's Overview tab; Performance (Ch 6) — a low rating on a competency-tagged KPI is the trigger the Phase-1 learning recommender will use, and completed courses appear in the calibration-room context for any manager weighing why a previously low rating has improved; Establishment (Ch 8) — grade-based competency requirements (a Grade-14 head of internal audit must hold the IA professional cert before stepping in) live in the establishment policy text today and are surfaced through certifications on the employee row; Recruitment (Ch 9) — new hires are intended to land on an "Onboarding" category of courses on day one, though the auto-assignment is not yet wired; Reports & Analytics (Ch 31) — the workforce-learning metrics on the institute-wide ISO 30414 export read from the same `enrolments` and `certifications` tables this chapter describes; Governance (Ch 27, in part) — certification expiry reminders are routed through the same `RecordAnalyticsEvent` listener as policy acknowledgements because both are evidence of statutory compliance.

## The data behind it

CIHRMS stores Learning & Development in five tables, all migrated in Wave 13 (May 2026) with a `reminder_sent_at` column added to certifications in Phase 5 (governance) work three weeks later.

- **`courses`** — one row per course. `id`, `title`, `slug` (auto-generated, unique), `description`, `category` enum, `format` enum, `provider` string, `cover_image` URL, `duration_minutes` integer, `price` decimal, `currency` (defaults to `GHS`), `skill_tags` JSON array, `is_published` boolean, `published_at` timestamp, `created_by` foreign key on `users` (`nullOnDelete`), timestamps, soft deletes. Indexed on `(is_published, category)` so the most common filter — "published courses in compliance" — is a covered scan.
- **`enrolments`** — one row per (course, employee) pair, enforced by a unique composite index. `course_id` and `employee_id` both `cascadeOnDelete`, `status` enum (Pending / Active / Completed / Abandoned / Expired), `progress_pct` decimal 0-100, `final_score` decimal 0-100, `certificate_path` string, four timestamps (`enrolled_at` defaulted to `useCurrent()`, `started_at`, `completed_at`, `last_activity_at`), timestamps, soft deletes. Indexed on `(employee_id, status)` so "my active enrolments" is a covered scan.
- **`certifications`** — one row per credential held by an employee. `employee_id` (`cascadeOnDelete`), `course_id` (`nullOnDelete` — a cert may or may not be linked to a course in CIHRMS, since most are external), `name`, `issuer`, `credential_id`, `issued_at` date, `expires_at` date, `document_path` string, `verification_url` string, `reminder_sent_at` timestamp (added in Phase 5), timestamps, soft deletes. Indexed on `(employee_id, expires_at)` so the per-employee expiry sort is fast, and on `reminder_sent_at` so the nightly job can find unreminded rows cheaply.
- **`skill_catalog`** — one row per skill the institute formally tracks. `name` (unique, 120 chars), `category` (40-char string from the same enum as the Add Skill form), `description`, timestamps. No foreign keys — the join to `EmployeeSkill` is by *name*, which is deliberate: a skill on a person's profile is allowed to predate its arrival in the catalogue, and the catalogue is allowed to outlive the last person who held a skill. Phase 1 considers tightening this into a real foreign key.
- **`employee_skills`** — one row per (employee, skill name) pair. `employee_id`, `name` (the same string the catalogue uses), `level` (`beginner` / `intermediate` / `advanced` / `expert`), `expires_at` date. Created by the listener below; editable by the employee from the Profile portal (Ch 16). Not soft-deleted — the row is the live skill, not a record of a past one.

What every reader of the screen needs to keep in mind:

- **Enrolment is per-course, not per-lesson.** CIHRMS does not store the lessons inside a course; it does not store videos, PDFs, links, SCORM packages, quizzes, or assessment results. `progress_pct` is a number the learner enters (or that an admin imports). The system trusts the report. Phase 3 ships the content layer; today the catalogue is a register, not a player.
- **The `skill_tags` column on a Course is a free-text JSON array.** It is not a foreign key into `skill_catalog`. Two courses tagged `"Procurement"` and `"procurement"` will create two different `EmployeeSkill` rows. The Phase-1 catalogue editor will tighten the tag input to autocomplete against the catalogue.
- **`final_score` is informational.** There is no pass-mark in the schema. A learner can complete a course at 12 % and the system records it; the question of "did they pass" is a manual one for now.
- **Certificates have expiry, not revocation.** `expires_at` is the only column that tracks the lifecycle of a credential. A revoked-mid-cycle cert has to be soft-deleted (`Certification` carries `SoftDeletes`) — the system does not carry a revocation reason column today.
- **Soft delete on every table.** Courses, enrolments, and certifications all carry `SoftDeletes`. A deleted enrolment is recoverable; the same DPA-redaction job (Ch 26) that wipes Employee personal data also wipes the linked Learning rows on a right-to-erasure request.
- **The system does not auto-enrol anybody.** Every enrolment row in the database was created by a person pressing a button — either the learner pressing **Enrol** on the catalogue card, or an HR admin issuing an enrolment in their stead. There is no "all employees are mandatorily enrolled in DPA training" pathway. See "What's missing".

## How it talks to other modules

- **`CourseEnrolled` event** → fired the moment an enrolment row is created by the service (with `wasRecentlyCreated` guarding the firstOrCreate against duplicate fires). No listener is registered today; the wiring exists so that Phase 1 can hang a "you've been enrolled" notification on the subject employee without touching the controller.
- **`CourseCompleted` event** → fired the moment an enrolment hits `Completed`. One listener picks it up: **`GrantSkillsOnCourseCompletion`** (queued on the `analytics` queue, auto-discovered by Laravel's listener scanner via the typed `handle()` parameter). It calls `LearningService::grantSkillsFromCourse($enrolment)`, which iterates the course's `skill_tags` and `firstOrCreate`s an `EmployeeSkill` row for each tag at level `intermediate`. The listener logs the number of new skills granted and swallows any throwable into a warning log line so a downstream failure cannot fail the completion call itself.
- **`CertificationIssued` event** → fired the moment a `Certification` row is created. Carries the actor User where one was supplied; today no listener is registered, so the event is a hook for Phase 1's "HR approved the cert" notification.
- **`CertificationExpiring` and `CertificationExpired` events** → dispatched by the **`governance:certification-reminders`** Artisan command, which is scheduled daily at 08:00 in `routes/console.php` and delegates to `GovernanceService::dispatchExpiryReminders($days)`. The service iterates `Certification::needingReminder($days)` in chunks of 100, picks the right event based on whether the expiry has already passed, stamps `reminder_sent_at = now()` so the row is not picked up again, and returns the count to the command. Both events are listened to by **`RecordAnalyticsEvent`** (registered explicitly in `AppServiceProvider::boot()`), which writes an `analytics_events` row so the institute-wide compliance dashboard can chart "how many certs expired this quarter" alongside policy acknowledgement rates.
- **Employees (Ch 3) → Profile Overview tab** reads `EmployeeSkill` rows through the same `skills` relation the Skills Matrix queries, so an employee who just completes a course sees the new skill on their own profile within the same worker tick the listener finishes.
- **Performance (Ch 6) calibration room** — managers walking into a calibration session can read each employee's skill list on the employee detail page (Overview tab). Phase 1 surfaces a per-employee "courses completed this cycle" panel on the Reviews page directly.
- **Establishment (Ch 8) grade-based competency** — the Phase-1 establishment work picks up `EmployeeSkill` rows against the `position_competency_requirements` table to flag people whose grade requires a skill they do not hold. The schema is in place; the report is not.
- **Reports & Analytics (Ch 31) workforce learning metrics** — total enrolments, completions per quarter, distinct learners, average score, and the count of expired certs per department all read directly from `enrolments` and `certifications`. The page is shipped; the export is Phase 1.

## What's missing in the MVP

A short, honest list — every item below is in the Phase-1 or Phase-3 backlog (Chapter 46):

- **No in-system content delivery.** No video player, no PDF viewer, no link wrapper, no SCORM player, no xAPI. A "self-paced" course is a row in `courses` with metadata about a thing the learner watches somewhere else. Phase 3's Learning Content Server adds the player; the catalogue and the progress tracker stay as they are.
- **No in-system quizzes or graded assessments.** `final_score` is a single number an admin (or the learner) can write into the row. There is no question bank, no per-attempt history, no anti-cheat, no proctoring. Phase 3.
- **No prerequisites enforcement.** The Course detail panel renders prerequisites if the resource returns any, but the `prerequisites` column is not yet on the `courses` migration and the enrolment service does not gate on it. Phase 1 adds the column; Phase 1 adds the gate.
- **No mandatory-training engine.** A course tagged "Compliance" is not automatically assigned to anybody. There is no rule engine that says "every new hire must complete Anti-Corruption (PSC) within 30 days" or "every staff member with `dept = Internal Audit` must hold the IA cert". Phase 1 wires this with a `MandatoryAssignment` rule table and a daily enforcement job.
- **No compliance dashboards by department / by mandatory course.** The Skills Matrix shows skill coverage; it does not yet show "what fraction of HR-required courses each department has completed". The data is there (`enrolments` joined to `courses` filtered by category); the page is Phase 1.
- **No auto-onboarding curriculum for new hires.** When HR creates an Employee row (Ch 3) the new hire is not auto-enrolled in any course. Phase 1 introduces an `auto_enroll_on_hire` flag on the Course model and an `EmployeeCreated` listener that walks the flag.
- **No recommender driven by Performance gaps.** A low rating on a competency-tagged KPI (Ch 6) does not surface a recommended course. The data shape is in place — Performance Review carries `performance_rating` per KPI; Course carries `skill_tags`; Employee carries `skills` — but no service reads it. Phase 1.
- **No certificate generation.** When a learner completes a course CIHRMS does not produce a PDF. The `certificate_path` column on `Enrolment` is for an externally-generated file an admin can attach. Phase 1 ships a `dompdf`-backed cert template (it's the same engine the Documents module already uses for letterheads).
- **No certificate revocation tracking.** Lifecycle is expiry-only; if a cert is revoked mid-cycle, soft-deletion is the workaround and the reason lives in HR's notes elsewhere. Phase 1 adds `revoked_at` and `revoked_reason` to the certifications table.
- **No SCORM, xAPI, LTI, or external-LMS sync.** No connectors. Phase 3 picks an open-source SCORM player (likely Articulate's open SCORM Cloud SDK) once the institute's Phase-1 internal-only workflows are stable.
- **No course-edit UI.** The route, the FormRequest, and the controller method exist; the catalogue card does not yet expose an "Edit" affordance. Phase 1's smallest item.
- **No dept_head scoping on the Skills Matrix.** A department head with `learning.manage` sees every employee in the institute, not just their team. The controller passes a `department_id` query parameter through but does not default it from the actor's headed departments. Phase 1.
- **Demo data.** There is no seeder for courses, enrolments, or certifications today. The catalogue is empty on a fresh database — the institute's first L&D admin populates it from a CSV import in onboarding, or the Phase-1 demo seeder shipped alongside it.

## Standards touchpoints

- **ISO 30414 §4.6 (training and development)** — the disclosures the standard recommends under §4.6 are: total training hours, training hours per FTE, training cost per FTE, % of employees with development plans, and completion rates for mandatory training. CIHRMS today provides the raw data for the first two (sum `enrolments.completed_at` × `courses.duration_minutes`, divide by headcount), and the data shape for the last (count `enrolments` whose course has `category = compliance`). The reporting page is shipped via the institute-wide analytics export (Ch 31). Forward to Chapter 44 for the line-by-line mapping.
- **Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843) §17, §18, §40** — a learner's enrolment history, progress data, scores, and certifications are personal data. The system relies on the employment-contract basis under §17; applies data-minimisation under §18 (no demographic columns on `enrolments`, no IP / device logging on progress updates); and supports right-to-erasure under §40 through the soft-delete on every Learning table — true erasure is handled by the redaction job that walks the linked Learning rows whenever an Employee row is redacted (Ch 26).
- **PSC training policy (Public Services Commission of Ghana, "Training and Development Policy for the Public Services of Ghana", revised 2019)** — mandates annual training for every public servant, prioritises induction within the first 12 months, and requires the parent body to maintain a register of all training undertaken. CIHRMS is that register. The mandatory-training engine that turns the policy from "maintain a list" into "enforce a list" is Phase 1 (see "What's missing").
- **WCAG 2.1 AA — learning content accessibility** — CIHRMS does not deliver content today, so the WCAG obligation in the MVP is restricted to the catalogue and progress pages themselves. The catalogue card uses semantic HTML (`<article>`, `<h3>`, `<button>`), every interactive control has a visible focus ring (the `:focus-visible` styles in the design system), every coloured pill carries its label as plain text alongside the colour cue, and the progress ring's percentage is repeated as a number in the centre of the ring rather than encoded only in the arc length. The bigger WCAG conversation — captions on videos, transcripts on audio, alt text on slide imagery — is in scope for Phase 3 when CIHRMS itself starts serving content.
- **Ghana Labour Act 2003 (Act 651) §10** — every worker has the right to "be trained and re-trained for the development of his or her skills". CIHRMS does not enforce a training entitlement (e.g. "every employee gets at least 16 hours of training per year"), but the data the analytics export produces lets HR demonstrate the institute is honouring §10 in aggregate. Forward to Chapter 44.

## What's planned next

Phase 1 of the government-grade roadmap (8-10 weeks, see Chapter 46) lands seven things directly on these screens: (1) the **mandatory-training engine** — a `MandatoryAssignment` rule table keyed on department, grade, role, or new-hire window, plus a daily enforcement job that creates the missing `Enrolment` rows and a per-department dashboard showing % complete vs target; (2) a **prerequisites column and gate** — adding `prerequisites` (JSON array of course IDs) to the `courses` table, surfacing it in the Publish panel, and blocking enrolment when the learner hasn't completed every prerequisite; (3) the **catalogue edit panel** — promoting the existing `UpdateCourseRequest` and `PATCH` route into a visible "Edit" affordance on every card, with the same `sometimes`-rule semantics that drove the Employee inline edits in Chapter 3; (4) the **certificate generator** — a `dompdf`-driven completion certificate (Course, learner name, final score, issue date, QR-coded verification link) written to `enrolments.certificate_path` automatically on `CourseCompleted`; (5) the **revocation lifecycle** — adding `revoked_at` and `revoked_reason` to the certifications table, surfacing a "Revoke" action in the My Learning certifications card for HR, and routing the dispatched `CertificationRevoked` event into the same analytics listener; (6) the **dept_head scoping on Skills Matrix** — defaulting `department_id` from the actor's headed-departments list so a department head who lands on `/learning/skills-matrix` sees their team, not the institute; (7) the **Performance → Learning recommender** — a per-employee panel on the Reviews page (Ch 6) that reads the lowest-rated `performance_rating` KPI, finds courses whose `skill_tags` overlap the KPI's competency tag, and offers a one-click "Enrol in {course}" button. Phase 3 (12-18 months out) ships the in-system content layer — video player, PDF viewer, SCORM 1.2 + xAPI tracking, a quiz engine with pass marks and proctoring, and a WCAG-2.1-AA accessibility pass on every piece of learning content.
