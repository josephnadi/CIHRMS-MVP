# CIHRMS Non-Technical Application Guide

## Purpose of this document

This guide explains CIHRMS in plain language for executives, department heads, HR officers, finance teams, auditors, project sponsors, trainers, and other non-technical stakeholders.

It describes what the system does, who uses it, and how each feature supports the daily work of CIHRM and similar institutions. It avoids software jargon and focuses on business value, user journeys, controls, and expected outcomes.

## What CIHRMS is

CIHRMS is an integrated Human Resource Management System for managing people, HR operations, governance, payroll, finance, communication, compliance, and member billing in one place.

The application brings together activities that are often handled through spreadsheets, paper files, email chains, isolated finance tools, and manual approvals. It gives staff and management one controlled platform where records can be created, reviewed, approved, tracked, reported on, and audited.

## Main stakeholder benefits

- Central employee and member records instead of scattered files.
- Faster approvals for leave, payroll, documents, payments, recruitment, and governance processes.
- Clear accountability because important actions are logged and traceable.
- Better compliance with HR policy, data protection, financial controls, and audit requirements.
- Self-service tools for employees, members, applicants, whistleblowers, and data subjects.
- Dashboards and reports that help managers make decisions from current information.
- Stronger security through roles, permissions, two-factor approval checks, private files, and signed integrations.

## Current application entry experience

The current landing page is a clean card-based portal selector rather than a long scrolling marketing page. Stakeholders and users first see the main workspaces available in CIHRMS and choose the card that matches their role or purpose.

The visible entry cards are:

- Admin / Super Admin for system control, users, roles, security, permissions, and audit oversight.
- HR Workspace for people operations such as employee records, recruitment, leave, attendance, performance, benefits, learning, and offboarding.
- Finance Hub for payroll, invoices, receipts, payments, reconciliation, journals, refunds, and other finance controls.
- Employee Portal for self-service activities such as profile updates, leave, attendance, tickets, documents, and learning.
- Member Portal for fees, invoices, statements, balances, receipts, and member payment options.
- Public Services for assisted or controlled access to public-facing services such as careers, complaints, data protection requests, whistleblower processes, kiosk access, and API information.

Each card is designed as a role-based doorway. Selecting a card sends the user to the appropriate login or portal entry path. The general staff login and access request buttons are placed under the left-side introduction so they remain available without distracting from the workspace cards.

## Main user groups

### Executive leadership

Executives use the system to monitor organizational health, view high-level HR and finance indicators, review audit information, and support governance decisions.

### HR administrators

HR teams manage employee records, departments, leave, recruitment, performance, benefits, offboarding, learning, attendance, and employee communications.

### Finance officers

Finance teams manage payroll, accounts, invoices, receipts, bank reconciliation, disbursements, Paystack payments, refunds, journal entries, and financial reporting.

### Department heads and managers

Managers review team information, approve requests, monitor performance, handle incidents, assign assets, and support staff development.

### Employees

Employees update their own profile, request leave, view attendance, raise tickets, participate in performance reviews, complete learning activities, receive notifications, and access relevant documents.

### Members

Members use the portal to view their profile, fees, invoices, statements, and payment options.

### Applicants and external users

Applicants can view vacancies and submit applications. External users can submit whistleblower reports, track complaints, and submit data protection requests where applicable.

### Auditors and compliance teams

Auditors can review logs, reports, approvals, data access, governance records, statutory outputs, and financial evidence packs.

## Application walkthrough

## 1. Welcome, login, and access control

Users enter the application through a card-based welcome page. The page presents the main workspaces, including Admin / Super Admin, HR Workspace, Finance Hub, Employee Portal, Member Portal, and Public Services. Users select the card that matches their role or purpose and are then sent to the correct login or portal entry path.

This matters because not every user should see or change the same information. CIHRMS separates access by role and permission so employees, managers, HR officers, finance officers, auditors, and administrators each see the right tools.

Key functions:

- Role-based landing cards for Admin / Super Admin, HR, Finance, Employee Portal, Member Portal, and Public Services.
- Staff login and access request actions from the landing page.
- User registration where enabled.
- Password reset and password change.
- Email verification.
- Two-factor security checks for sensitive actions.
- Single Sign-On support for organizations that want login through an external identity provider.
- Permission-based access to modules and actions.

## 2. Dashboard

After login, users land on a dashboard that summarizes important activity across the organization.

The dashboard gives management and staff a quick overview without searching through every module.

Key functions:

- View key HR, payroll, finance, attendance, recruitment, and activity indicators.
- See recent system activity.
- Access common modules quickly.
- Support leadership visibility into operational health.

## 3. Employee records

HR teams maintain employee records, while employees can manage approved parts of their own profile.

The employee record is the foundation of the system. Payroll, leave, attendance, benefits, learning, assets, performance, and reporting all depend on accurate employee information.

Key functions:

- View employee directory.
- Create new employee profiles.
- Update employee personal, employment, contact, emergency, bank, and status information.
- Store employee documents privately.
- Upload and manage employee profile photos.
- Add and remove employee skills.
- View individual employee profiles.
- Remove employees when appropriate, while preserving audit needs.
- Maintain departments and department details.
- Generate secure temporary file links for employee photos and documents.

## 4. Profile self-service

Key functions:

## 47. Typical end-to-end workflows

### Hiring a new employee

1. HR publishes a job.
2. Applicant applies through the careers page.
3. HR reviews the applicant.
4. Applicant status is updated.
5. Offer is sent for signing where required.
6. Successful applicant becomes an employee record.
7. Employee receives profile, department, role, and onboarding information.

### Managing leave

1. Employee submits leave request.
2. Manager or HR reviews request.
3. Leave is approved or rejected.
4. Balance and status are updated.
5. The decision becomes part of the record.

### Processing payroll

1. Payroll run is created.
2. Payroll is calculated.
3. Authorized reviewer approves it.
4. Payments and payslips are generated.
5. Payroll is marked paid.
6. Statutory files and exports are produced.

### Routing a document for approval

1. User uploads or composes a document.
2. User chooses recipients and required actions.
3. Recipients review, sign, approve, or reject.
4. Document status updates automatically.
5. Final document can be downloaded with controls and audit history.

### Collecting member fees

1. Admin creates fee products.
2. Billing run generates invoices.
3. Member views invoice in the portal.
4. Member pays online or through supported channels.
5. Paystack confirms payment.
6. Receipt and invoice status are updated.

### Reconciling bank transactions

1. Finance imports bank statement.
2. System suggests matches.
3. Finance confirms, links, unlinks, or rematches lines.
4. Adjustments are posted where needed.
5. Reconciliation report is printed or reviewed.

### Handling a privacy request

1. Data subject submits request.
2. Email verification confirms the request.
3. DPO reviews and acknowledges it.
4. Request is fulfilled, rejected, or withdrawn.
5. Export or erasure evidence is retained as appropriate.

## 49. What managers should know

- CIHRMS is not just an HR filing system; it is a workflow and control platform.
- The system is built around approvals, permissions, and audit trails.
- Many modules connect to each other, so good data entry in one area improves outcomes elsewhere.
- Finance and HR actions can require additional security checks.
- Public-facing features are limited and controlled, while staff features require login.
- Reports are only as useful as the records maintained in the system.

## 51. Glossary

- Approval: A decision by an authorized person to accept, reject, or move a request forward.
- Audit log: A record of important actions taken in the system.
- Benefit: A staff entitlement such as a plan, claim, dependant, or e-card.
- Billing run: A process that creates invoices for members based on configured fees.
- Calibration: A management review process used to align performance ratings.
- Disbursement: A payment sent out through a bank, ACH, or mobile money channel.
- Journal entry: A finance record showing how money is posted in the accounts.
- Kiosk: A shared attendance device or screen.
- Member portal: A self-service area for members to view fees, invoices, and statements.
- Performance Improvement Plan: A structured plan to support an employee whose performance needs improvement.
- Reconciliation: Matching bank statement activity against system records.
- Signed link: A secure temporary link used to access private files.
- Two-factor check: An extra security confirmation before sensitive actions.
- Webhook: A secure message sent between CIHRMS and another system when something happens.
